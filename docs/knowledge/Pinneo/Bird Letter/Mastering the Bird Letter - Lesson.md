Envelopes are something we’ve evolved over the years. The gold standard for envelopes is a 5x7 colored envelope. Period. End of story. It’s basically the size of a birthday card. Also, if you are an agent or a broker, you must include your disclosure language in the letter. Be upfront and transparent. **[45:00] Structure of the Bird Letter: Paragraph One (Empathy)**
**[Shauna Pinneo]**
The objective of the bird letter is to put yourself in the space of the person receiving it. It's an empathy thing. What is their impression going to be? You have to figure out what they want to hear. Women are generally better at this empathy piece than men. Men can sometimes be a bit abrupt and just lay out their demands. If you're a guy, have a female friend or partner read your letter.

The first paragraph needs to set the stage. It needs to say something warm and personal about you, why you are contacting them, and why you are drawn to their neighborhood. It is *not* the place to list your buying criteria. Do not say, "I've identified your house as fitting my investing criteria." That is rude and offensive. People's homes are personal. Start softly. "I've always loved the Queen Anne neighborhood because of the Wednesday farmer's market..." Warm it up. Make them feel like you are not a production line. 

**[55:00] Paragraph Two & Three: Intentions and Call to Action**
**[Shauna Pinneo]**
The second paragraph is where you lay out your aims and intentions. This is where you convey your business persona: your ethics, your integrity, your problem-solving ability. The third paragraph is your call to action. Keep it low pressure. Thank them for their time and let them know how to reach you. 

Do not include a logo on personal bird letters. If you are mailing to multi-family owners, a subtle, professional logo is fine, but for personal residences, logos scream "corporation" and kill the personal touch.

Get a secondary number just for bird letter responses. The greeting should be concise and professional: "Hello, you've reached Shauna. ## Chapter Title: Mastering the Bird Letter: The Architecture of Direct Mail Deal Flow

### The "Ism" (Philosophy)
**The First Impression and Empathetic Deliberation**
The "bird letter" (direct mail marketing) is your absolute first point of negotiation. If the recipient does not open your envelope, the phone will never ring. To master direct mail, you must master *empathy*. You must mentally place yourself in the exact space, place, and emotional position of the property owner receiving your letter. Real leverage begins before you ever speak to the seller; it is established by crafting a letter that disarms their defenses, combats negative investor stereotypes, and compels them to reach out. 

### Core Concepts
**1. Targeting High-Demand Areas**
Selection of the "Farm Area" must be highly deliberate. Target high-demand locations—places where people actively want to live. Furthermore, keep these locations within a reasonable driving distance from your home to maintain a tight pulse on property management, zoning laws, price per square foot, and neighborhood dynamics.

**2. Building the "Farm Package"**
Your "Farm Package" is the customized list of property owners you intend to mail. This data is pulled from a local Title Company or public county records. 
*   **The Criteria:** Request a list of every multi-family property (or single-family, depending on your goal) that falls into the *lower one-third of tax-assessed values* for that neighborhood. 

**3. The Aesthetic of the Mailer**
If the mailer looks like corporate junk, it goes in the trash. The physical architecture of the letter is critical to its open rate:
*   **The Envelope:** The gold standard is a 5x7 colored envelope (the size of a standard birthday card). Never use plain white business envelopes.
*   **The Postage:** Always use a large, live, picturesque commemorative stamp. *Never use metered mail.* Metered mail instantly flags the letter as a mass-marketing solicitation.
*   **Addressing:** Envelopes must be hand-addressed. Do not use printed labels. 
*   **Paper Quality:** Use heavy cardstock if possible, or a minimum of 24lb paper. Anything lighter feels cheap.
*   **Formatting:** Have your copy center cut the 8.5x11 paper down so it folds perfectly into the 5x7 envelope without awkward margins. Use a clean, legible font (Size 12 to 14). 

**4. The Three-Paragraph Structure**
The content of the letter must be concise (never more than one page) and structured strategically:
*   **Paragraph 1 (The Warm Intro):** Establish a personal connection. Speak to why you love their specific neighborhood (e.g., local parks, farmers markets). *Do not* list your buying criteria here. 
*   **Paragraph 2 (Aims and Intentions):** Establish your business persona. Clearly state your ethics, integrity, and problem-solving abilities. Explain that you buy properties in "as-is" condition and want to make the process mutually beneficial. 
*   **Paragraph 3 (The Call to Action):** A low-pressure invitation. Thank them for their time and clearly state how they can reach you. 

### The Toolbox
*   **The Farm Package:** The targeted data list pulled from a Title Company.
*   **Live Commemorative Stamps:** A psychological tool to make the mail feel personal and manually curated.
*   **Agent/Broker Disclosure:** Mandatory, transparent language included in the letter if the sender holds a real estate license, framed positively.
*   **Dedicated Voicemail Line:** A separate phone number exclusively for bird letter responses, featuring a highly professional, concise greeting that matches the persona of the letter.

### Case Studies
*   **The "Investor Criteria" Misstep:** Shauna notes that a common mistake is opening a letter by saying, "I've identified your house as fitting my investing criteria." This offends sellers because a home is deeply personal, not just a line item on a spreadsheet. Letters structured this way generate defensive, angry responses rather than collaborative ones.
*   **The Authentic Russian Investor:** A student with English as a second language sent a letter with several endearing grammatical errors. While Shauna's instinct was to heavily edit it, the letter actually succeeded because it was *authentic*. When sellers called the number, the voice on the phone matched the charming, genuine tone of the letter. The lesson: The letter must authentically reflect the person answering the phone.

### Action Items
*   **Define Your Farm Area:** Identify a high-demand neighborhood within driving distance and learn its comps, rents, and zoning.
*   **Pull Your List:** Contact a Title Company to pull a list of properties in the lower 1/3 of tax-assessed values in your target area.
*   **Procure Supplies:** Purchase 5x7 colored envelopes, heavy cardstock/24lb paper, and live commemorative stamps. 
*   **Draft the Letter:** Write a strict three-paragraph letter (Intro, Intentions, Call to Action) and have a trusted peer give you brutal, honest feedback on its tone.
*   **Set Up Infrastructure:** Establish a dedicated secondary phone line with a professional voicemail greeting promising a 24-hour callback. 
*   **Automate the Labor:** Hire a student or retiree to hand-address the envelopes to ensure consistency and free up your time for negotiation.
