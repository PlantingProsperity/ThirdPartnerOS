## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Substitution of Security
*   **Trigger:** IF [The Maker (Borrower) wishes to sell a property collateralized by a seller-carry note] AND [The Maker wishes to retain the specific financing terms/cash proceeds].
*   **Action:** THEN [Invoke the Substitution of Security Clause to transfer the Deed of Trust to a different property].
*   **The Math:**
    The substitute property must meet **BOTH** criteria:
    1.  $$Equity_{Substitute} \ge Principal_{Outstanding}$$
    2.  $$NetCashFlow_{Substitute} \ge Payment_{Committed}$$
    *(Where "Payment" is the minimum committed amount, usually interest-only).*
*   **Why:** This divorces the debt from the specific "dirt," allowing the investor to walk away from a sale with 100% of the proceeds (tax-free capital availability) while preserving low-interest, long-term debt.

### Rule: Note Splitting (The "Too Big to Move" Fix)
*   **Trigger:** IF [The note to be moved is too large for the equity/cash flow of any *single* substitute property].
*   **Action:** THEN [Negotiate to split the single Promissory Note into two or more smaller notes, creating new Deeds of Trust against multiple substitute properties].
*   **The Math:** $$Note_{Total} = Note_A (Property_1) + Note_B (Property_2)$$
*   **Why:** Allows the preservation of a large financing asset by spreading the risk across the portfolio rather than cashing it out.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** **The Mandatory Legal Clause**
> *This must be inserted into the Promissory Note and Deed of Trust Addendum during the initial purchase negotiation.*
> **Verbatim Script:**
> "Maker, at Maker's option, reserves the right to substitute the collateral for the note to a different piece of real property under the following two conditions:
>
> Condition number one: That the Equity in the substitute property is equal to or greater than the outstanding principal balance on the promissory note at the time of substitution.
>
> AND, Condition number two: That the Net Positive Cash Flow on the substitute property is equal to or greater than the minimum amount of committed payment on the promissory note at the time of substitution."
> **Tone:** Legal, precise, non-negotiable (once agreed).

> **Scenario:** **The Late Negotiation Warning**
> *Explaining why you cannot ask for substitution rights after the deal is signed.*
> **Verbatim Script:**
> "It's not something you want to come back five or ten years later and say, 'Oh by the way, instead of cashing you out, do you mind if I simply substitute the collateral for this note?' The leverage that you have with that seller happens in the negotiation of the purchase of their property."
> **Tone:** Cautionary, strategic.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Green Avenue Flip (Substitution)
    *   **Situation:** Investor owns "123 Brown Ave" (6-Plex, $600k Value, $400k Debt, $200k Equity). Investor buys "123 Green Ave" (House) with seller financing ($200k Note to Mr. Green).
    *   **Strategy Used:** Investor fixes and sells Green Ave for $300k. Instead of paying off Mr. Green, investor invokes **Substitution of Security**, moving Mr. Green's $200k note to the $200k equity in Brown Ave (2nd position).
    *   **Outcome:** Investor sells Green Ave, pays $0 to lenders, and keeps ~$270k cash proceeds.
    *   **Key Lesson:** Substitution allows you to harvest 100% of sale proceeds as cash while keeping the debt alive.

*   **Case Name:** The Brown Avenue Exit (Note Splitting)
    *   **Situation:** Years later, Investor sells Brown Ave (Value $800k). Owes Mr. Brown ($400k) and Mr. Green ($200k). Needs to move the debt to access the cash for a "Bird" (new deal).
    *   **Strategy Used:**
        1. Move Mr. Green ($200k) to a new property with sufficient equity.
        2. **Split** Mr. Brown's note ($400k) into two $200k notes because no single property has $400k equity. Secure them against two different properties.
    *   **Outcome:** Sold Brown Ave for $800k, cashed out nobody, walked away with ~$720k cash for the next acquisition.
    *   **Key Lesson:** If a note is too big to fit on one property, split it to fit your portfolio's available equity pockets.
