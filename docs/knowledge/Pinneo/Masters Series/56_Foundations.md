## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Solution Mode Switch (The Apollo Protocol)
*   **Trigger:** IF [Catastrophic Failure / Crisis occurs]
*   **Action:** THEN [Immediately switch from "Problem Analysis" to "Solution Execution"]
*   **The Script:** "Work the solution, not the problem."
*   **Why:** Analyzing the problem creates paralysis. Moving to the solution creates momentum.

### Rule: The Passion Sustainability Formula
*   **Trigger:** IF [Starting a new business entity]
*   **Action:** THEN [Audit the primary motivator]
*   **The Math:**
    *   IF motivator == Money/Carrot $\rightarrow$ Probability of Success = $\frac{1}{1000}$ (0.1%)
    *   IF motivator == Passion $\rightarrow$ Probability of Failure = 0% ("There is no such thing as failure")
*   **Why:** When you start with passion, the drudgery (dialing for dollars, maintenance) becomes play. When you start with money, the work becomes unsustainable toil.

### Rule: The Vacuum of Leadership
*   **Trigger:** IF [You refuse to commiserate or complain about a tragedy]
*   **Action:** THEN [You create a vacuum that draws people/resources in]
*   **Why:** People are repelled by victimhood but magnetically attracted to strength and positivity ("That which is likened to itself is drawn").

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** **Life is an Emergency**
*   **Mindset:** "Confronting your own mortality alleviates the fear of death and lends clarity of purpose."
*   **Application:** Operate as if the timeline is short. Do not set the thermostat to 74 degrees. Attack goals immediately rather than assuming a 75-year runway.

*   **Principle:** **The Gratitude Inventory**
*   **Mindset:** "I bemoaned the moment because I had no shoes, and then before me, a man who had no feet."
*   **Application:** When taking a hit, immediately inventory what remains (e.g., "I lost my left leg, but I have my right leg, eyes, and intellect"). This prevents the slide into depression.

*   **Principle:** **Visualizing Point Z**
*   **Mindset:** Do not look at the obstacle (Point A to B). Look at the feeling of the result (Point Z).
*   **Application:** Brady did not focus on learning to walk (the struggle); he focused on the feeling of standing on Mt. Rainier (the result).
