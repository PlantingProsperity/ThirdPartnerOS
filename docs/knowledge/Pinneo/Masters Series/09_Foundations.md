## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Pre-Site Triad
*   **Trigger:** IF preparing to view a multi-family property.
*   **Action:** THEN establish three baselines before arrival: 1. Current Rental Market Value (Subjective), 2. Sold Comparisons (Cap Rates), 3. Price Per Unit/Square Foot.
*   **Why:** You must know if a deal is good instantly upon arrival; statistics do not tell the story of "why" one unit rents for more than another.

### Rule: The Potential Rent Valuation
*   **Trigger:** IF a seller claims rents are low but "can be raised easily."
*   **Action:** THEN value the property based strictly on *current* actual income streams.
*   **Why:** If rents could be raised easily, the seller would have done it. If you take the risk to execute the turnaround, you must keep the equity created.

### Rule: The Dirt Value Calculation
*   **Trigger:** IF analyzing a property with land (e.g., a 4-plex).
*   **Action:** THEN divide Total Lot Sq Ft by Zoning Density (e.g., 7,200 / 800 = 9 units).
*   **The Math:** $Lot Size \div Density Requirement = Potential Units$
*   **Why:** The value of the "dirt" (potential 9 units) may exceed the value of the existing structure (4 units).

### Rule: The Water Main Detective
*   **Trigger:** IF inspecting an older building with potential galvanized plumbing.
*   **Action:** THEN look for a concrete saw-cut/patch forming a path from the street meter to the building.
*   **Why:** A patch indicates the line was replaced. No patch implies the original galvanized line is still there, representing a potential $3,000–$30,000 liability.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Seller's Reflection
*   **Mindset:** "The condition of the property is a direct reflection of the attitude of the seller."
*   **Application:** If you see small deferred maintenance (peeling paint, broken latches), assume the invisible expensive systems (plumbing, electrical) are also neglected.

*   **Principle:** Manage the Keys, Don't Let Keys Manage You
*   **Mindset:** Efficiency in turnover is critical.
*   **Application:** Use a Master Key System where only the cylinder is changed (5 seconds), not the lockset.

*   **Principle:** Income Stream Granularity (A La Carte)
*   **Mindset:** Everything is a separate product. Rent is for housing. Parking is separate. Storage is separate.
*   **Application:** Never bundle amenities. 22 parking spaces x $75/mo = massive increase in building value (Cap Rate).

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The 38-Unit Parking War
*   **Situation:** Greg bought a 38-unit building with only 22 parking spaces. Tenants were used to parking first-come, first-served for free.
*   **Strategy Used:** Implemented "Paid Reserved" parking ($75/mo). Enforced via "Impoundment" using concrete-filled barrels and chains rather than towing.
*   **Outcome:** Tenants learned responsibility. Generated significant monthly income ($1,650/mo) which translated to over $300k in asset value at a 6% Cap.
*   **Key Lesson:** Don't give revenue to a tow truck driver. Control the asset and the penalty revenue.

*   **Case Name:** The L3 Zoning Discovery
*   **Situation:** Inspecting a 4-plex on a 7,200 sq ft lot in Seattle.
*   **Strategy Used:** Applied zoning knowledge (L3 = 1 unit per 800 sq ft). $7,200 / 800 = 9 units.
*   **Outcome:** Identified the property was sitting on a 9-unit site, making the land more valuable than the 4-plex income.
*   **Key Lesson:** Analyze the "Structure on Dirt" separately from the "Dirt" itself.

*   **Case Name:** The Deck Over Living Space
*   **Situation:** Inspecting buildings with lanais/decks.
*   **Strategy Used:** Visual check: Is there finished square footage (living room/ceiling) underneath the deck?
*   **Outcome:** Rejection or heavy discount. "It’s not if it leaks, but when."
*   **Key Lesson:** Flat roofs/decks over living space in wet climates are fatal flaws.
