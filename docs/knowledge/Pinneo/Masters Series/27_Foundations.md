## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Physics of Decision
*   **Trigger:** IF you are "sitting on a fence" regarding a deal or life choice.
*   **Action:**
    1.  Ask: "What more information do I need to act?" -> Go get it immediately.
    2.  IF no more information is needed -> THEN ask: "Why aren't I doing something?" (Identify Fear).
    3.  Commit to a decision, even if that decision is "Do Nothing."
*   **Why:** "We can't steer a parked car." Life unfolds only while in motion. A proactive decision to wait is valid; a passive state of waiting is useless.

### Rule: The Short Sale Valuation (Net REO Calculation)
*   **Trigger:** IF determining the offer price for a Short Sale.
*   **The Math:**
    $$ \text{Offer Price} \approx (\text{Market Value}) - (\text{Legal Costs} + \text{Eviction} + \text{Holding Costs} + \text{Repairs} + \text{Resale Commissions} + \text{Closing Costs}) + \text{Small Premium} $$
*   **Action:** Construct a "Net REO Sheet" listing every cost the bank will incur if they foreclose (Attorney fees, insurance, taxes, vacancy security, 6% agent fees).
*   **Strategy:** Offer slightly *more* than their Net REO result.
*   **Why:** To prove to the bank, purely on numbers, that taking your cash now yields a higher net result than the "road of pain" (foreclosure).

### Rule: The "Zero Fluff" PSA
*   **Trigger:** IF writing a Purchase and Sale Agreement for a bank Short Sale.
*   **Action:** The Buyer (Investor) pays all closing costs *except* transfer taxes and title insurance.
*   **Constraint:** Do not put any "fluff" or cash-out on the seller's side.
*   **Why:** The bank is taking a loss; they will not approve a deal where the defaulting borrower receives value or the net number is diluted by buyer-side fees.

### Rule: The Contractor Bid Triangulation
*   **Trigger:** IF presenting repair estimates to a bank.
*   **Condition:** You have three bids (e.g., $15k, $20k, $25k).
*   **Action:** Submit *all three* physical bids in the package, but use the **middle** number ($20k) on your Net REO summary sheet.
*   **Why:** It demonstrates reasonableness and credibility to the committee.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** "The Unforgiving Minute" (via Dr. Seuss's *The Waiting Place*)
*   **Mindset:** Time spent in indecision is the only true waste. Even a decision to "do nothing," if made proactively, frees up mental RAM.
*   **Application:** If you decide not to return a call, make that a firm decision ("I am not returning this"). Do not let it linger as an "I should do this."

*   **Principle:** Dual Vision (The Entrepreneur vs. The Bank)
*   **Mindset:** You must view the asset through two incompatible lenses simultaneously.
    1.  **Wholesale:** Buy low, sell high (profit margin).
    2.  **Loss Mitigation:** Mitigate the bleeding (stop the loss).
*   **Application:** When negotiating, do not discuss "potential." Discuss "liability, risk, and holding costs."

*   **Principle:** Humanizing the Bureaucracy
*   **Mindset:** The bank is a cold machine, but the Loss Mitigator is a stressed human (likely promoted from collections) with hundreds of files.
*   **Application:** "Get them in the front door, but help them get out the back door." Structure your package to make *their* job easy, allowing them to look good to their superiors.
