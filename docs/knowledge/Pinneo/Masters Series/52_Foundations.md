## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Reverse-Engineered Timeline
*   **Trigger:** IF you want to retire at specific Age $X$ and are currently Age $Y$.
*   **Action:** THEN negotiate a financing term (amortization) equal to exactly $X - Y$.
*   **The Math:** $$ \text{Loan Term} = \text{Target Retirement Age} - \text{Current Age} $$
*   **Why:** This ensures the asset becomes a free-and-clear cash cow exactly when the income is needed, converting debt service into spendable income.

### Rule: The Power of Ten (Capital Aggregation)
*   **Trigger:** IF a commercial asset requires a down payment too large for one person (e.g., $150,000).
*   **Action:** THEN form an LLC with 10 entities (you + 9 others), splitting the down payment requirement equally.
*   **The Math:** $$ \text{Individual Contribution} = \frac{\text{Total Down Payment}}{10} $$
*   **Why:** Reduces barrier to entry ($150k becomes $15k) and mitigates individual risk while acquiring high-value assets.

### Rule: The Self-Sustaining Buy Box
*   **Trigger:** IF evaluating a property for a retirement plan.
*   **Action:** THEN the current market rents must cover Operating Expenses + The Aggressive Amortization Payment.
*   **The Math:** $$ \text{Gross Rent} - 30\% \text{ Exp} - \text{Mortgage Payment} \ge \$0 $$
*   **Why:** The plan fails if the investors have to feed the property monthly. The tenant must pay off the retirement debt.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** **The Unforgiving Math of Insurance**
*   **Mindset:** Insurance annuities are bets against your own life; the insurer wins if you die early. Real Estate annuities are bets *on* the asset; your family wins regardless of when you die.
*   **Application:** Avoid financial products that terminate on death. Build systems that transfer wealth to heirs.

*   **Principle:** **Living Deliberately (Thoreau)**
*   **Mindset:** "We must live in the present, launch yourself on a wave, and find your eternity in each moment."
*   **Application:** Do not passively hope for retirement. Actively construct the vehicle (the wave) that carries you there.

*   **Principle:** **The "Finish Line" Fallacy**
*   **Mindset:** Most people start plans but change course when things get tough or boring.
*   **Application:** Apply the "Fight to the Spear." Success is not in the starting; it is in the ability to endure the "last hour's toil."

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The 10-Unit Freedom Fund
*   **Situation:** An investor (age 45) wants to retire at 65. They identify a 10-unit building for $1.5M ($150k/unit). They lack the $150k down payment.
*   **Strategy Used:**
    1.  **Syndication:** Formed an LLC with 9 other 45-year-olds. Each contributed $15,000 (Total $150k down).
    2.  **Financing:** Seller Carry-back for $1.35M at 5% interest.
    3.  **Amortization:** Negotiated a specific **20-year term** to match the retirement date.
*   **Outcome:**
    *   **Years 1-20:** Rents ($13k) covered Expenses ($3.9k) and Debt Service ($8.9k). Cash flow was near zero (break-even).
    *   **Year 20:** Debt is $0. Rents increased to $18k. Net income is $12,600/month distributed 10 ways ($1,260/month per person).
*   **Key Lesson:** Sacrifice cash flow today to pay down principal aggressively. The goal is not income now; it is maximum, unencumbered income at the specific target date.

*   **Case Name:** The McDonald's Retiree
*   **Situation:** Greg met a 77-year-old man applying for a job at McDonald's.
*   **Strategy Used:** Relied on a money manager/stocks for retirement.
*   **Outcome:** Market dropped ("stocks dropped off the table"), income disappeared, forced to work for basic necessities.
*   **Key Lesson:** "I don't want to be that guy." Control your own retirement vehicle; do not abdicate responsibility to third-party managers.
