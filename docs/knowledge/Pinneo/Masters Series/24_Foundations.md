## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The 1031 Timeline (The Unforgiving Clock)
*   **Trigger:** Closing of the Relinquished Property (Day 0).
*   **Action:** 
    1.  **Identify** Replacement Property by Day 45 (Written, signed, specific).
    2.  **Close** on Replacement Property by Day 180.
*   **The Math:** $T_{close} + 45 \text{ days} = \text{ID Deadline}$; $T_{close} + 180 \text{ days} = \text{Purchase Deadline}$. (Note: Periods run concurrently, not consecutively).
*   **Why:** The IRS allows no extensions (except for rare disasters like 9/11). Missing a deadline by one day disqualifies the exchange.

### Rule: Identification Limits (The 3-Property Rule)
*   **Trigger:** Submitting the 45-day identification paper to the QI.
*   **Action:** You may identify up to **three** properties of unlimited value.
*   **Condition:** IF identifying > 3 properties, THEN the total value cannot exceed 200% of the sold property's value (The 200% Rule).
*   **Exception:** IF violating both above rules, the exchange is valid ONLY IF you acquire 95% of the value of all identified properties.

### Rule: The Reverse Exchange
*   **Trigger:** You find the "deal of a lifetime" (Replacement) *before* selling your current asset (Relinquished).
*   **Action:** The QI sets up an "Exchange Accommodation Titleholder" (EAT) to hold title to one of the properties. You cannot hold title to both simultaneously.
*   **Why:** To secure a high-velocity deal without waiting for a sale, despite higher fees.

### Rule: The Section 121 Conversion (Rental to Residence)
*   **Trigger:** Moving into a property acquired via a 1031 Exchange.
*   **Action:** 
    1.  Rent property for 12+ months (establish investment intent).
    2.  Move in and use as Primary Residence for 2+ years.
    3.  Own the property for a total of 5 years.
*   **The Math:** $\text{Gain Exclusion} = \$250,000 \text{ (Single)} / \$500,000 \text{ (Married)}$.
*   **Why:** Converts tax-deferred gain into tax-free money legally.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** **Subjective Intent vs. Objective Actions**
*   **Mindset:** The IRS cannot read your mind, so they judge your intent based on your actions.
*   **Application:** If you buy a vacation home via 1031 and move in the next week, your "objective action" proves your "subjective intent" was personal use, not investment. You must create a paper trail (Schedule E) that proves investment intent first.

*   **Principle:** **The Abnormal Path**
*   **Mindset:** "Winning is not normal." The average person stops when it gets hard or technical. The winner pays the "price of admission" (learning the tax code, paying for expert counsel) to achieve results the crowd deems impossible.

*   **Principle:** **Economic Sense > Tax Avoidance**
*   **Mindset:** Never buy a bad building just to save on taxes. It is better to pay the capital gains tax on a profitable sale than to lose your principal on a rushed, inferior purchase.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Starker Decision
*   **Situation:** A timber company wanted Mr. Starker's land but Starker didn't have a replacement property yet.
*   **Strategy Used:** The company put the cash in a trust for Starker to use later.
*   **Outcome:** The IRS sued and lost. The "Starker Exchange" (Delayed Exchange) was born and later codified in 1986.
*   **Key Lesson:** Precedent can be set by those willing to challenge the interpretation of "exchange."

*   **Case Name:** The Panic Buyer
*   **Situation:** Investor sells for $600k net. Waits until Day 40 to look for a replacement.
*   **Strategy Used:** Rushes to buy an inferior property just to save the $75k tax bill.
*   **Outcome:** Ends up with a property that loses money or value, costing far more than the tax would have been.
*   **Key Lesson:** Do not let the tax tail wag the investment dog. Plan ahead or pay the tax.

*   **Case Name:** The Meat Market Split
*   **Situation:** Owner selling a business including a building and butcher equipment.
*   **Strategy Used:** Bifurcated 1031 Exchange.
*   **Outcome:** Real Estate (Building) exchanged for a Hotel; Personal Property (Equipment) exchanged for Espresso Bars.
*   **Key Lesson:** You can exchange personal property (equipment) separately from real estate.
