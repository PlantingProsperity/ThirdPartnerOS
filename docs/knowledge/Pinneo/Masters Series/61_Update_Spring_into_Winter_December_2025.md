## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lettuce and Tomatoes** | The "whole salad" of clauses, terms, and considerations in a note (e.g., SOS, no prepay penalty) that accompany the price. Often worth paying a higher price to secure. | Deal Structuring |
| **If/Then Mindset** | A preventative mental algorithm where one anticipates failure points (e.g., refinancing unavailability) and structures the solution ("Then") into the initial contract. | Risk Management |
| **Expandability** | The ability to create new equity by altering the legal description or use of an asset (e.g., condo overlay, zoning changes) rather than relying on market appreciation. | Valuation |
| **Substitution of Security (SOS)** | A clause allowing the borrower to move the collateral of a note to a different asset, keeping the favorable debt terms alive while freeing the original property. | Financing |
| **First Position Lien Slot** | A financing position created by "condo-izing" a large building into smaller units, allowing for smaller, private loans (e.g., Self-Directed IRAs) instead of one large institutional loan. | Capital Raising |
| **The Bank of You** | Treating secured, low-interest, long-term debt (controlled via SOS) as your own personal cost of funds to lend out or deploy elsewhere at a margin. | Arbitrage |
| **Slice and Dice** | A clause or strategy permitting the division of a property or note, often used in conjunction with condo overlays or partial releases. | Collateral |
| **Life Estate** | A deal structure where the seller retains the right to inhabit the property until death or move-out, often in exchange for favorable purchase terms (no payments/interest). | Vendor Financing |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Lettuce & Tomato Trade-Off
* **Trigger:** IF [Structuring a purchase offer and need better terms/time]
* **Action:** THEN [Offer a higher Purchase Price or offer to give up some Equity]
* **The Math:** `Higher Price + (SOS + Long Term + No Personal Guarantee) > Low Price + Short Term`
* **Why:** "Cash flow always supersedes equity... I'll pay a little more to secure a longer-term runway."

### Rule: Negotiating Private Interest Rates
* **Trigger:** IF [Negotiating the interest rate on a seller-carry note]
* **Action:** THEN [Ask seller to bring their current CD, Bond, or Money Market statements to the meeting]
* **The Math:** `Offer Rate > Seller's Current CD Rate` (e.g., If CD is 1%, offer 3.5% - 4%)
* **Why:** "If banks can get away with offering that to billions of dollars, why is it that we think we can't negotiate that on a private note?"

### Rule: The "All Cash" Installment Plan
* **Trigger:** IF [Seller demands "All Cash"]
* **Action:** THEN [Offer "All Cash" split into two funding events: 50% now via new first position loan, 50% later via short-term note]
* **The Math:** `Purchase = (50% LTV Bank Loan) + (50% Seller Note @ 0% Interest due in 24 months)`
* **Why:** Avoids high hard money costs (12% + points) by using low LTV bank money; seller still gets their price, just timed differently.

### Rule: The Condo Overlay Liquidity Play
* **Trigger:** IF [Acquiring/holding a large multi-unit building (e.g., 60 units)]
* **Action:** THEN [Condo-ize the building to create individual legal descriptions]
* **The Math:** `1 Building = 1 Lien Slot (Institutional)` vs `60 Units = 60 Lien Slots (Private/IRA)`
* **Why:** "First position lien slots mean the world of financing opens up everywhere... I replace that money with self-directed IRA money... at 5% and 6%."

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **The Three-Dimensional Game**
* **Mindset:** Amateurs play 2D (Buy/Sell or Buy/Rent). Power Players play 3D by becoming the Lender.
* **Application:** Use the "Principle of Expandability" and "The Bank of You." Don't just own the dirt; own the debt structure and the ability to move that debt to new dirt.

* **Principle:** **"Can Be" vs. "Is"**
* **Mindset:** Never value a property based on its current state. Value it based on its zoning potential (Comprehensive Plan).
* **Application:** Look at a single house and see three houses + two DADUs. "Understanding not what is, but what can be there."

* **Principle:** **The Stingray Standard**
* **Mindset:** Don't accept the "standard bicycle" (the easy outcome) just to be done. Hunt for the specific "green Stingray with ram horns" (the perfect deal/outcome) even if it takes years.
* **Application:** "To leave it at wrong is worse than it was in the first place... We get to find a way to get to a better place."

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Triplex Expandability
* **Situation:** Purchase of two triplexes for $1.1 million.
* **Strategy Used:** $50k down, $1.05M note at 4% for 20 years. Included Substitution of Security (SOS) and "slice and dice" clauses.
* **Outcome:** Greg/Student controls ~$2M of debt at 4% that has "legs" (can be moved).
* **Key Lesson:** The debt itself is an asset ("The Bank of You"). Use SOS to move this cheap capital to larger commercial deals later.

* **Case Name:** Queen Anne Life Estate
* **Situation:** Elderly woman, house-rich but cash-poor, unable to pay taxes ($215k strike price).
* **Strategy Used:** Life Estate. $15k down. $200k note with **no payments, no interest**. Seller lives rent-free until death/move-out. Greg pays taxes/insurance.
* **Outcome:** She lived there 11.5 years (avoided care facility). Greg sold for $1.17M. His basis was ~$300k (purchase + taxes/ins).
* **Key Lesson:** Solve the seller's human problem (staying in home) to secure zero-cost leverage.

* **Case Name:** The Stingray Bicycle
* **Situation:** 8-year-old Greg earned half for a specific rare bike; Dad forced him to buy a generic one. Greg felt it "wasn't right."
* **Strategy Used:** "The Hunt." Decades later, used modern tools (marketplace/internet) to track down the exact rare 1968 model.
* **Outcome:** Bought the bike immediately without negotiation. Hangs it in office as a reminder.
* **Key Lesson:** "None of us think correctly. We need change... But to leave it at wrong is worse than it was in the first place." Finish the business.
