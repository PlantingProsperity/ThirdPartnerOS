## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Quarterly Summit Protocol
*   **Trigger:** Quarterly payment cycle for any private note holder (Blue Ribbon Investor).
*   **Action:** Schedule a face-to-face meeting (do not mail the check). Arrive dressed professionally with a "Summit Binder."
*   **The Deliverable:**
    1.  **Payment History:** Show consistency (highlight Principal is safe even if Interest Only).
    2.  **Collateral Update:** Photos of the property, recent improvements, curb appeal.
    3.  **Financials:** Current income/expense report (Proof of DSCR).
    4.  **Security:** Paid tax receipts and Insurance Binder naming them Loss Payee.
*   **Why:** To contrast the tangibility and safety of this investment against the volatility of their other paper assets (stocks/bonds).

### Rule: The "Invisible Questions" Algorithm
*   **Trigger:** During the Quarterly Summit Meeting, while reviewing the portfolio.
*   **Action:** Ask two specific questions organically:
    1.  *Comparison:* "Of your other investments... are they performing at a steady, predictable interest rate? Or are they volatile? Is your stockbroker taking you to lunch?"
    2.  *Referral:* "Do you know of anybody else who is considering selling their property that you would like to have the kind of experience that you’re having with me?"
*   **Why:** To migrate the investor's remaining capital (the "Oak Tree") into your control and access their social network.

### Rule: The Town Car Strategy (Substitution Execution)
*   **Trigger:** IF you need an investor to perform a hassle task (e.g., sign a reconveyance/Substitution of Security at escrow).
*   **Action:**
    1.  Send a Town Car to pick them up from work.
    2.  Wait for them during the signing.
    3.  Drive them back to work.
    4.  Place flowers and a $200 dinner gift certificate on their desk for their return.
*   **Why:** Transforms a bureaucratic annoyance into a "Four Seasons" experience that generates referrals from coworkers.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Opportunity of Attention.
*   **Mindset:** We live in a "multitasking menu system" world. Giving someone 100% eye-contact attention is a rare commodity that builds instant trust.
*   **Application:** When meeting a seller, say "You have my full attention" and mean it. No phones, no distractions.

*   **Principle:** Relationship Capital > Cash Capital.
*   **Mindset:** A transaction is not an exit; it is an entry. A seller carry-back note is an "interview" for the rest of that person's net worth.
*   **Application:** Treat a $5,000 note holder with the same reverence as a $1M investor because the $5,000 is just the "seed."

*   **Principle:** The Four Seasons Standard.
*   **Mindset:** "Whatever you need, we will do it." No excuses. High fees (or high trust) are justified by anticipating needs before they are voiced.
*   **Application:** Never make an investor ask for paperwork or updates; over-deliver before the question is asked.
