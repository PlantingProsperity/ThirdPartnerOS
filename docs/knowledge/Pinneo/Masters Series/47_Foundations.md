## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Anchor Subsidy
* **Trigger:** IF negotiating with a primary Anchor Tenant (e.g., Department Store, Grocery).
* **Action:** THEN accept minimal base rent (e.g., $2/ft vs. $15/ft market rate), build their parking, and accept modified Triple Net terms.
* **The Math:** `Anchor Rent << Market Rent` (Anchor is a loss leader).
* **Why:** The Anchor provides the "Critical Mass" of traffic. Without the Anchor, you cannot attract the "Fill-in" tenants who pay the high rents.

### Rule: The Parking Ratio
* **Trigger:** IF designing or analyzing a retail project.
* **Action:** THEN apply specific ratios per use type, not just code minimums.
    *   **Restaurants:** 10 spaces per 1,000 sq ft GLA (Gross Leasable Area).
    *   **General Retail:** 3 spaces per 1,000 sq ft GLA.
* **Why:** A deal lives or dies on parking. Suburban retail fails if customers cannot conveniently park.

### Rule: Commission Risk Mitigation
* **Trigger:** IF paying a leasing commission to a broker.
* **Action:** THEN split the payment: 50% upon lease signing, 50% upon tenant opening for business.
* **The Math:** `Total Commission = (3% of Base Rent Yrs 1-5) + (1.5% of Base Rent Yrs 6-10)`
* **Why:** Protects the landlord if the tenant signs, starts construction, but runs out of money/defaults before opening.

### Rule: The Pre-Lease Threshold
* **Trigger:** IF seeking construction financing for a new retail center.
* **Action:** THEN secure pre-leases for at least 50% of the project (specifically the Anchor or major user).
* **Why:** Lenders require proof of concept and guaranteed revenue before releasing funds in tight credit markets.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** The Accountability of Knowledge
* **Mindset:** "You can only be accountable for what you know." Innocent mistakes (ignorance) differ from willful mistakes (knowing better).
* **Application:** When entering a high-stakes niche like Commercial Retail, "innocent" ignorance is not a defense against financial ruin. You must hire consultants to bridge the gap between what you know and what you don't.

* **Principle:** Begin at the Beginning
* **Mindset:** Forward planning dictates function. You cannot shoehorn a specific use (like a coffee shop with wet trash) into a generic space after it is built.
* **Application:** Involve leasing consultants during the architectural design phase (pre-checkbook) to ensure logistics (loading, trash, venting) match target tenants.

* **Principle:** The Counter-Intuitive Cluster (Co-Tenancy)
* **Mindset:** Competitors should be neighbors.
* **Application:** Don't fear proximity to rivals. High-end brands (Cartier, Tiffany) cluster to create a "destination." One store verifies the demographic for the other.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The San Antonio Critical Mass
* **Situation:** Developing a new project that needs to attract shoppers immediately.
* **Strategy Used:** Establish a "triad" of anchors: A Movie Theater (night/weekend draw), a Specialty Grocery (daily needs draw), and a cluster of restaurants.
* **Outcome:** Created "Critical Mass."
* **Key Lesson:** Different anchors feed the center at different times; the diversity creates the volume necessary to support the smaller retailers.

* **Case Name:** The Seattle "Polygon"
* **Situation:** Defining a trade area for a Seattle retail location using standard "radius rings" (1-mile, 3-mile).
* **Strategy Used:** Abandoned rings for "Polygons."
* **Specifics:** Because of water and hills, a 3-mile ring might include fish in the Puget Sound. They drew custom shapes along the I-5 corridor (North/South) rather than East/West.
* **Key Lesson:** Standard demographics fail in geographies with natural barriers. You must map the actual drive-time/access capability.
