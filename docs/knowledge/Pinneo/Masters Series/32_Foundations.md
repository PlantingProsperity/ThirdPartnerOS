## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Condominium** | Not a type of building, but strictly a *form of ownership*. It changes ownership from one owner per parcel to multiple owners per parcel without changing zoning, use, or density. | Legal Description |
| **There are no accidents** | The belief that outcomes (good or bad) are the result of attending to or ignoring components; "accidents" are merely a lack of "eyes of opportunity" to see what is converging in front of you. | Mindset |
| **Income Approach** | Valuing a property based on NOI and Cap Rate. This is the "Wholesale" buying price for an apartment building. | Valuation |
| **Comparative Market Approach** | Valuing a property based on the sales price of individual units. This is the "Retail" selling price for a converted condo. | Valuation |
| **Limited Common Elements** | Assets owned by the association but assigned to a specific unit (e.g., parking, storage). Can be used as closing incentives or profit centers. | HOA Structure |
| **Public Offering Statement** | The mandatory disclosure document (survey, declaration, budget) required before you can legally give tenants a notice of termination or offer units for sale. | Due Diligence |
| **Wrap Policy** | A specific, expensive insurance product for condo construction that supersedes individual contractor insurance, required because standard policies exclude condo liability. | Risk Management |
| **Sheetrock In** | The typical physical boundary of a condominium unit owner's property; the owner owns the air space and finish, the association owns the structure. | Surveying |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Gourley Conversion Margin Formula
*   **Trigger:** IF evaluating a building for potential conversion to condos.
*   **The Math:**
    $$ \text{Retail Value} - 13\% (\text{Commissions/Closing}) - \text{Hard Costs} - \text{Soft Costs} = \text{Net Profit} $$
    *   *Constraint:* IF Net Profit < $20,000 per door, THEN DO NOT PROCEED.
*   **Why:** You need a sufficient buffer for risk. $20k is Craig Gourley's "walk away" number.

### Rule: The "Touch It, Warrant It" Doctrine
*   **Trigger:** IF deciding to renovate a unit during conversion (specifically in WA).
*   **Action:** THEN assume a 4-year warranty of habitability on any system you modify.
*   **Why:** Statutory liability creates a "tail" where developers can be sued for defects years later.

### Rule: The Pre-Close Entitlement Strategy
*   **Trigger:** IF the seller is cooperative during the purchasing phase.
*   **Action:** THEN gain access to perform the Survey, Engineering, and draft the Declaration/Public Offering Statement *before* closing.
*   **Why:** To minimize soft costs (interest carry). Ideally, be ready to sell within 24 hours of taking title.

### Rule: The "Cash for Keys" Arbitrage
*   **Trigger:** IF you need a tenant out to renovate, but they have 90 days legal notice.
*   **Action:** THEN offer 100% deposit refund + Cash Bonus (e.g., $2,000) + "No Cleaning Required."
*   **The Math:** Compare [Daily Holding Cost x Days Saved] vs. [Payout Amount].
*   **Why:** "I'll make it worth your while to move early." The time saved on interest carry usually dwarfs the cash payout.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** "There are no accidents, only inattention."
*   **Mindset:** We perceive events as accidents only because we were inattentive to the components converging to make them happen. The answers are always right in front of us if we have "eyes of opportunity."
*   **Application:** When a deal fails or succeeds, do not attribute it to luck. Trace back the specific details you noticed or ignored.

*   **Principle:** Form vs. Use
*   **Mindset:** Do not let the physical structure dictate the business model.
*   **Application:** A mini-storage, a parking garage, or a marina can be "condominium-ized." You can sell every square tile in a room to a different owner if the paperwork supports it.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Parking Lot Arbitrage
*   **Situation:** A 4-unit apartment building had 16 parking spaces in a dense area.
*   **Strategy Used:** Converted the building to condos. Designated parking as Limited Common Elements. Sold the 4 units, but sold the surplus 12 parking spaces separately/assigned to specific buyers.
*   **Outcome:** The net sales gain of the parking exceeded the value of the 4 housing units.
*   **Key Lesson:** Look for value in the "non-living" space. Dissect the asset into its most valuable components.

*   **Case Name:** The Dinner Party Termination
*   **Situation:** Converting a building and needing to serve the required Notice of Termination and Public Offering Statement (POS) to tenants.
*   **Strategy Used:** Hosted a dinner party with wine, invited the lender, and handed out the "bad news" (termination) paired with the "good news" (opportunity to buy).
*   **Outcome:** Sold three units that very night.
*   **Key Lesson:** Control the environment when delivering legal notices. Turn a disruption into a sales event.

*   **Case Name:** The 12-Building March
*   **Situation:** Large multi-building project with existing tenants.
*   **Strategy Used:** Paid tenants to move from front buildings to back buildings. Renovated front buildings first, then "marched back" through the project.
*   **Outcome:** maintained cash flow (occupancy) while renovating in phases.
*   **Key Lesson:** Use tenant mobility to balance cash flow against construction speed.
