## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Director Decision** | A specific administrative variance process that avoids public hearings/neighbor opinions. Used to bypass standard code restrictions by negotiating directly with city staff based on merit. | Bureaucracy, Variance |
| **The "Hug" Strategy** | The act of embracing a property's restriction (e.g., wetlands) rather than fighting it. By improving the restriction (mitigation), you gain leverage to reduce setbacks. | Negotiation, Development |
| **Buffer Averaging** | A mechanism to reduce a required environmental setback (e.g., from 100ft to 50ft) in exchange for ecological enhancement elsewhere on the site. | Land Use, Wetlands |
| **Advanced Framing** | A construction technique (24" on center, 2-stud corners) used not just for structure, but to reduce lumber costs and maximize insulation cavity space. | Green Construction, Cost Control |
| **Solar Ready** | A loophole to achieve a 5-Star Built Green rating without the cost of panels. Wiring the house for future solar is sufficient to jump from 4 to 5 stars. | Value Add, Appraising |
| **LID (Low Impact Development)** | Managing stormwater on-site (infiltration) rather than tying into city sewers. Used as a bargaining chip to save infrastructure costs and gain city favor. | Site Work, Politics |
| **Volatile Organic Compound (VOC)** | Chemicals in standard paint/carpets that "off-gas." Avoiding these (Low/No VOC) is a key marketing differentiator for health-conscious buyers. | Marketing, Health |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Wetland Equity Swap
* **Trigger:** IF land is rendered unbuildable by environmental setbacks (e.g., 100ft wetland buffer).
* **Action:** THEN propose a "Mitigation Plan" (remove invasive species, plant natives) in exchange for a "Special Permission Director Decision" to reduce the buffer.
* **The Math:** `Mitigation Cost (Plants/Labor) < Value of Created Buildable Lot ($150k)`
* **Why:** 98% of investors run from wetlands. By embracing the restriction, you create equity ($300k in this case) out of "useless" dirt.

### Rule: The Green Premium Calculation
* **Trigger:** IF deciding between Standard Code Construction vs. Green Construction.
* **Action:** THEN choose Green if the market supports the premium.
* **The Math:** `Construction Cost (+10%) vs. Sale Price Premium + Tax Credits ($2k) + Marketing Match ($1k) + Faster Sale Velocity`
* **Why:** Building to code is building the "worst house legally allowed." Green future-proofs the asset and attracts a less price-sensitive, highly motivated buyer pool.

### Rule: The "Motion First" Principle
* **Trigger:** IF you have a project but the exit strategy (Sell vs. Hold) is unclear.
* **Action:** THEN commence development immediately and decide the exit later.
* **The Math:** `Time in Motion > Time in Analysis`
* **Why:** "We're not sure which [strategy] we're going to pick... but that's not going to stop the motion process." Motion creates options; stagnation kills deals.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **"Know HUD Better Than HUD Knows HUD"**
* **Mindset:** Do not rely on city officials to tell you what is allowed. They are often uneducated on niche codes (like Green or LID). You must educate *them*.
* **Application:** When the City of Tukwila said the land was unbuildable, the developer found the specific clauses (Director Decision) and taught the city how to apply them, eventually becoming the city's example case.

* **Principle:** **The Alchemy of Restriction**
* **Mindset:** "Restrictions are where the competition dies and the equity lives."
* **Application:** Most see a steep slope or a creek as a liability. The Pinneo investor sees these as barriers to entry that protect their profit margins once solved.

* **Principle:** **Make No Small Plans**
* **Mindset:** "There is nothing in small plans to stir the blood of men."
* **Application:** Do not just build a house; build a prototype that changes the city code. High ambition fuels the persistence required to survive 30+ city meetings.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Tukwila Wetlands Project
* **Situation:** Brie Campbell bought a fourplex that included a "bonus" steep slope with a creek. City code required a 100ft buffer, making the land legally unbuildable (buffer extended into neighbor's yard).
* **Strategy Used:** **Special Permission Director Decision**. Used "Buffer Averaging." Brie offered to remove blackberries and plant 850 native plants (mitigation) in exchange for reducing the setback to 50ft.
* **Outcome:** The variance created two legal buildable lots valued at $150,000 each (Total $300,000 equity created from thin air).
* **Construction:** Built a 2,187 sq ft Green Home. Cost ~$330,000. Listed for $549,950.
* **Key Lesson:** Do not accept the "No" from the counter. Educate the bureaucrat on the obscure parts of their own code (LID/Green) to get the "Yes."
