## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Daily Trinity (Do/Call List)
*   **Trigger:** Start of every business day.
*   **Action:** Create three headers on paper: **Product (Birds)**, **Money**, **Buyers**. Under each, create two columns: **Do** and **Call**.
*   **Constraint:** Every activity must fall under these headers. If it does not, "put it to rout."

### Rule: The Buy & Flip Valuation Formula
*   **Trigger:** Analyzing a potential flip property.
*   **The Math:**
    $$ \text{Acquisition Price} + (30\% \text{ of Acquisition Price}) + \text{Closing Costs (In)} + \text{Remodel Costs} + \text{Holding Costs} + \text{Selling Costs (10\%)} = \text{Total Basis} $$
*   **Decision Gate:** IF [Total Basis] <= [Market Value for 60-day Sale], THEN [Buy]. ELSE [Walk Away].
*   **Why:** The 30% margin is not for profit; it is a safety net for uncontrollable variables (rates, economy, mistakes).

### Rule: The Remodel Estimation Heuristic
*   **Trigger:** Estimating rehab costs for a flip.
*   **Action:** IF [Rookie Investor], THEN [Estimate Costs] * 2 + $10,000.
*   **Why:** Remodeling never costs less than you think.

### Rule: The Direct Mail Ratio
*   **Trigger:** Sending "I want to buy your house" letters to owners in the lower third of value.
*   **The Math:** 1,000 Letters = 5 to 10 Phone Calls.
*   **Why:** Proactive volume is required to find the "Bird."

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** **The Great Equalizer**
*   **Mindset:** No one gets a head start on time. Everyone has 24 hours. The winner is determined by the intolerance for movements that produce nothing.
*   **Application:** Convert the car into a "Rolling University" and ensure every phone call ends with a Question of Proposition.

*   **Principle:** **Toyota vs. Maserati**
*   **Mindset:** Trade in the lower half of values. "I want to know that what I buy, clean up and sell, that I’ve got a market for that. I’ve got a 'normal person' market."
*   **Application:** Ignore the million-dollar homes for flips; focus on bread-and-butter housing.

*   **Principle:** **Cash vs. Wealth**
*   **Mindset:** "You will not generate wealth buying and flipping real estate. You will generate cash."
*   **Application:** Use flips to serve the habit of holding real estate long-term (Wealth generation).

*   **Principle:** **The Pie Philosophy**
*   **Mindset:** "A piece of the pie is better than going without dessert."
*   **Application:** Bring in partners/money even if it splits profits, because 50% of a deal is better than 100% of no deal.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** Converting a social call (fishing story) into a Private Money pitch.
> **Verbatim Script:** "Let me be direct. Because there’s no reason I should be giving 6% interest to banks when I can be giving it to my blue ribbon investors like you, Jim. Do you have any money in the bank that’s not getting 6%?"
> **Tone:** Direct, mutually beneficial, transitioning from casual to business.

> **Scenario:** Overcoming the "penalties" objection on a CD/Savings account.
> **Verbatim Script:** "Oh, that week of interest at two and three quarters percent on your $45,000. In other words, the price of a Happy Meal?"
> **Tone:** Perspective-shifting, slightly humorous but logical.

> **Scenario:** Direct Mail Letter to Homeowners (The "Toyota" Market).
> **Verbatim Script:** "Hi, my name’s Greg. I just love your house... I realize it doesn’t have a For Sale sign out in front, but boy I would love the opportunity to purchase this from you. If you have any interest at all in selling your home, would you give me a call? I won’t waste your time. I’m very nice to talk to... I have a three-year-old that I’d love to put through college someday. And this is for her future."
> **Tone:** Personal, vulnerable, non-threatening, distinctly *not* a corporate solicitation.

> **Scenario:** Pitching a deal to a Pre-Sold Buyer.
> **Verbatim Script:** "Are you a buyer of this product in this location with this rent roll, with this upside condo conversion potential? Are you a buyer for this product if I can deliver it to you for $2 million? Yes or no."
> **Tone:** Binary, efficient, high-level business.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** Kermit the Frog
*   **Situation:** A vacant, neglected house near Green Lake (Seattle). Owner lived in Chicago.
*   **Strategy Used:** "Pheasant Hunting" (called title company, traced owner). Established value via exterior Sold Comps ($100k offer sight unseen barring "crazy" interior). 90-minute phone negotiation convinced owner to fly out.
*   **Outcome:** Bought for $100k + $6k rehab. Sold in 1 day for $175k. Net profit ~$45k.
*   **Key Lesson:** Knowledge of Sold Comps allows for confident, rapid offers (sight unseen) that motivate sellers to act.

*   **Case Name:** The Salmon Fisherman (Jim)
*   **Situation:** Investor friend returned from a trip. Greg needed capital.
*   **Strategy Used:** "The Unforgiving Minute." Greg drove to see the salmon picture (ego stroke) specifically to get face-to-face to ask for the money.
*   **Outcome:** Secured $45,000 at 6% interest (which was sitting in a bank CD earning 2.75%).
*   **Key Lesson:** "There are no accidents." Use social reasons to get face-to-face, but always have a "Question of Proposition."

*   **Case Name:** The Tenant Buyer
*   **Situation:** Greg was flipping a house.
*   **Strategy Used:** Identified a buyer *before* finishing the flip by looking at his existing rental tenant pool.
*   **Outcome:** Sold the flip to the tenant, saving holding costs and commissions.
*   **Key Lesson:** Your buyers are often already in your database (tenants).
