## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Assumption of the Close
* **Trigger:** IF the seller is wavering or considering a competitor's offer.
* **Action:** THEN frame the offer as a completed logic statement focusing on their benefit, not the price.
* **The Script:** "I can assume that if I wrote you the same type offer... but the offer was [Higher Price]... I'm assuming that that's a better deal for you."
* **Why:** It shifts the psychological burden from "making a decision" to "accepting a benefit" (e.g., fishing trips, new boat).

### Rule: The Credibility Instant Check
* **Trigger:** IF a seller questions your ability to close or your identity.
* **Action:** THEN immediately offer to put a reference on the phone right then and there.
* **The Script:** "You tell me what kind [of reference] you want and I'll get somebody on the phone right now... What about a banker? Okay, let me get a banker on the phone."
* **Why:** Aggressive transparency eliminates doubt faster than documents.

### Rule: The Rights Walk-Away
* **Trigger:** IF a counter-party demands a restrictive covenant (e.g., a gag order, a restriction on talking to press/authors).
* **Action:** THEN walk away or refuse the condition, even if the price is agreed upon.
* **The Math:** $Deal + Restricted Freedom < No Deal.
* **Why:** "They didn't just want the house; they wanted my freedom." Surrendering rights establishes weakness and violates the principle of Living Deliberately.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **The 60-Second Window**
* **Mindset:** Real estate is not a spectator sport. There are moments when the "physics" of a deal change from courtship to decision. You often have less than a minute to commit to an offer.
* **Application:** You must "pencil" the deal in your mind before the meeting. When the moment comes, you do not say "I need to check"; you say "Yes."

* **Principle:** **Structure on Dirt vs. Story on Dirt**
* **Mindset:** Sometimes the value isn't in the rental income or the utility of the building, but in the narrative (International news story, resistance against developers).
* **Application:** Monetize the story (e.g., selling tiles for Credo Square) rather than the square footage.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Edith Macefield House (Credo Square)
* **Situation:** The elderly owner refused to sell to developers, leaving a small house surrounded by a commercial complex. After her death, the executor (Barry) had a $300k offer from developers that included a "gag order."
* **Strategy Used:**
    1. **Assumption of the Close:** Offered $310k ($10k premium) framed as "fishing trips."
    2. **Certainty:** $10k non-refundable earnest money, no contingencies, Form 17 waiver.
    3. **Principle Stand:** Refused the developer's gag order to preserve rights.
    4. **Vision:** Plan to lift the house 30ft on steel beams and sell "Credo Tiles" to the public to fund it.
* **Outcome:** Secured the purchase contract for $310k against the developers.
* **Key Lesson:** "Ammo at the Ready" allows you to strike in the 60-second window. A seller will often choose certainty and freedom over a corporate offer with restrictive strings.
