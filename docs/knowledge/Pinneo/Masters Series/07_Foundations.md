## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wrap Note (AITD)** | A financing tool where the seller creates a new note that "wraps" around an existing underlying note. The goal is to make money on the "margin" (interest spread) rather than the equity. | Arbitrage / Banking |
| **Dirt and Bones** | The physical real estate asset (land and structure). Pinneo prefers making money on terms/paper over making money on "dirt and bones." | Hard Assets |
| **Base Hits** | Consistent, repeatable deals that generate monthly cash flow (e.g., $959/mo), as opposed to swinging for "Home Runs" (large one-time equity checks). | Investment Philosophy |
| **Self-Unemployed** | A badge of honor for the entrepreneur who wakes up daily to match wits against obstacles, free from a traditional job. | Entrepreneurship |
| **Searching for Waldo** | The daily act of hunting for opportunities in the marketplace. | Deal Finding |
| **Freedom** | Strictly defined as **Cash Flow** that replaces the need to work. It is not Net Worth. | Wealth Definition |
| **Lock-out / Prepayment Penalty** | A clause preventing the buyer from paying off the principal early, protecting the investor's long-term yield spread. | Deal Structure |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Wrap Acquisition Criteria
*   **Trigger:** IF searching for a property suitable for a Wrap/AITD strategy.
*   **Action:** THEN target properties with High Equity (Free and Clear is best) and negotiate specific terms.
*   **The Checklist:**
    1.  **High Note Amount:** Seller carries back large portion of price ($400k on $425k purchase).
    2.  **Low Interest Rate:** Negotiate hard for 4% - 5%.
    3.  **No Due on Sale:** The underlying note MUST NOT have a Due on Sale clause.
    4.  **Long Term:** 20-30 years before cash-out.
*   **Why:** You need a stable, low-cost underlying debt to arbitrage against a higher-rate retail buyer.

### Rule: The Wrap Sale Structure (The Spread)
*   **Trigger:** IF selling a property via All-Inclusive Trust Deed (AITD).
*   **Action:** THEN structure the sale to maximize the margin between the underlying note and the incoming note.
*   **The Math:**
    *   **Price:** Market Value (or exactly what you paid).
    *   **Down Payment:** Enough to cover your initial down payment + closing costs.
    *   **Interest Rate:** Underlying Rate + ~3% (e.g., Buy at 5%, Sell at 7.875%).
    *   **Term:** Underlying Term minus 6 months.
*   **Why:** To create a "bank-like" spread where profit comes from the difference in interest rates ($1,451/mo spread in the example).

### Rule: The "Interest Only" Mandate
*   **Trigger:** IF setting terms for the person buying *from* you.
*   **Action:** THEN require **Interest Only** payments.
*   **Why:** 
    1. Lowers the buyer's monthly payment (affordability).
    2. Keeps the principal balance high, maximizing the interest spread income for the investor.
    3. You do *not* want them to pay down principal; you want the income stream.

### Rule: The Safety Net (Escrow)
*   **Trigger:** IF finalizing the payment mechanics of a Wrap Note.
*   **Action:** THEN utilize a Third-Party Escrow Account.
*   **Process:** Buyer pays Escrow -> Escrow pays underlying Note/Taxes/Insurance -> Escrow sends difference (profit) to Pinneo.
*   **Why:** Guarantees the buyer that the underlying mortgage is being paid, and protects the investor by ensuring taxes/insurance are covered.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Banker's Mindset
*   **Mindset:** "Banks don't make money working; they make money on margin."
*   **Application:** Stop trying to get rich by flipping "dirt and bones." Become the bank. Borrow at 5%, lend at 8%. The difference is wealth.

*   **Principle:** The Unbroken Line (James Michener)
*   **Mindset:** "The master in the art of living makes little distinction between his work and his play... He simply pursues his vision of excellence at whatever he does."
*   **Application:** If you distinguish between "work time" and "play time," you will not succeed. Passion must be the center.

*   **Principle:** Freedom vs. Net Worth
*   **Mindset:** "Freedom is not Net Worth. Freedom is Cash Flow."
*   **Application:** Net worth creates leverage; Cash flow creates the ability to quit a job. Structure deals for monthly income, not just balance sheet growth.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** Single Family "Base Hit" Wrap
*   **Situation:** Greg buys a house for $425k and sells it for the exact same price, making money only on the financing terms.
*   **Strategy Used:** The Wrap Note / AITD.
    *   *Buy:* $425k Price, $25k Down, $400k Note @ 5% (Interest Only). Payment: $1,666/mo.
    *   *Sell:* $425k Price, $25k Down, $400k Note @ 7.875% (Interest Only). Payment: $2,625/mo.
*   **Outcome:** Monthly Net Profit: **$959**. Total 30-year profit: ~$250,000+ (without compounding).
*   **Key Lesson:** You can make a fortune selling a property for exactly what you paid for it if you control the interest rate spread.

*   **Case Name:** The 10-Plex Multiplex Wrap
*   **Situation:** Purchasing a 10-unit building with seller financing and reselling quickly.
*   **Strategy Used:** Value-add (laundry, rent bumps) + Wrap Note resale.
    *   *Buy:* $1.1M Price, $100k Down, $1M Note @ 5%. Payment: $4,166/mo.
    *   *Sell:* $1.3M Price, $200k Down (recoups initial inv + rehab), $1.1M Note @ 7.875%. Payment: $7,218/mo.
*   **Outcome:** Monthly Net Profit: **$3,052**.
*   **Key Lesson:** Multiplexes offer greater scale for AITD strategies; one deal can replace a median salary.
