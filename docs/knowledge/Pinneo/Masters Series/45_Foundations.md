## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Tri-Level Exit Formula
* **Trigger:** Evaluating ANY new acquisition or project.
* **Action:** Structure the deal to pass three distinct stress tests in specific order.
    1.  **Plan A (Keep):** Does it pencil as a long-term rental with current financing?
    2.  **Plan B (Option):** Can I increase cash flow via Lease Option consideration?
    3.  **Plan C (Sell):** Only if A and B work, do I look at the profit spread for a sale.
* **The Math:** `If (Rental Income - Expenses - Debt Service) < 0, THEN REJECT DEAL (regardless of potential flip profit).`
* **Why:** "Sell" is a vulnerability. You cannot control interest rates or market shifts. You must be able to wait out the storm.

### Rule: The Pilot’s Financing Protocol
* **Trigger:** Negotiating short-term or construction financing.
* **Action:** MUST negotiate a "Roll-to-Permanent" clause or an automatic extension provision.
* **Condition:** If using Private Money: Negotiate that if "Sell" fails, the debt converts to equity/ownership percentage to wait out the market.
* **Why:** To prevent foreclosure when a short-term note matures during a market downturn.

### Rule: The Profit Spending Prohibition
* **Trigger:** Receiving construction draws or holding interim funds.
* **Action:** NEVER use project funds for personal living expenses.
* **Verbatim Mandate:** "Don't spend profit until you receive profit."
* **Why:** Borrowing future profits creates an over-leveraged asset. If the project stalls, you have eaten your seed corn and cannot service the debt.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** The Job Site Fence Paradox
* **Mindset:** We build fences to feel safe ("Violators Will Be Prosecuted"), but fences prevent us from connecting with the people (lenders/partners) we need.
* **Application:** In negotiation, remove the "perfect expert" mask. Admit vulnerability. Show the "doom" plan. This humility builds stronger trust than false confidence.

* **Principle:** The Unforgiving Minute (Expect the Unexpected)
* **Mindset:** Markets change overnight (9/11, Subprime crash). Standard variance is not an anomaly; it is the default state.
* **Application:** "I don't have a crystal ball. Nor do you." Therefore, systems must be redundant.

* **Principle:** Responsibility over Momentum
* **Mindset:** "Life isn't an emergency." It is better to keep a day job for a decade to build a portfolio responsibly than to jump "cold turkey" and drown your investors.
* **Application:** Build a transition plan (5-10 years) rather than relying on "deal-to-deal" survival.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Dream Home Fence
* **Situation:** Greg spent 6 years building a home surrounded by an ugly, aggressive 8ft security fence.
* **Strategy Used:** On the final day, the fence was removed in 15 minutes.
* **Outcome:** The property instantly transformed from "Dangerous/Keep Out" to "Open/Beautiful."
* **Key Lesson:** In business, we often keep our fences up too long. Vulnerability (taking the fence down) reveals the true value of the deal/person.

* **Case Name:** The Hunting Outhouse
* **Situation:** A hunter waits all day with a gun ready; nothing happens. The moment he sets the gun down to use the outhouse, the game appears.
* **Strategy Used:** (Retrospective) Learned to "Expect the Unexpected."
* **Outcome:** Understanding that "markets turn" exactly when you are least prepared.
* **Key Lesson:** You cannot time the market. You must be prepared for the event to happen when you are "with your pants down."

* **Case Name:** The "Paper Rich" Flipper (Cautionary Tale)
* **Situation:** Investor quits job, buys a remodel project, uses construction loans to pay for groceries/mortgage (living expenses).
* **Strategy Used:** Plan C only (Sell). Borrowing future profits.
* **Outcome:** Market shifts, financing dries up, investor cannot refinance because they have no income/cash reserves. Foreclosure.
* **Key Lesson:** Don't rob the project fund to live on. Keep a job until the *portfolio* (not the next flip) pays for your life.
