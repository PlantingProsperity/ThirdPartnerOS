## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The 4 Ways Lenders Make Money
*   **Trigger:** IF you are operating as "The Bank of You" or structuring a loan.
*   **Action:** THEN you must structure the deal to profit from four distinct avenues.
    1.  **Margin:** The spread (e.g., Borrow at 6%, Lend at 9%).
    2.  **Fees:** Setup fees/Points (1 point = 1% of loan).
    3.  **Consequences:** Penalties for specific behaviors (Prepayment penalties, late fees).
    4.  **Services:** Ongoing charges (Servicing fees, Assumption fees, Extension fees).
*   **Why:** Banks maximize yield by extracting profit at every stage of the loan lifecycle, not just interest.

### Rule: The Capital Creation Loop (Liquidity Play)
*   **Trigger:** IF you have high equity in an income property ("Dead Equity") AND need liquid cash to start a lending business.
*   **Action:**
    1.  Buy a break-even property (SFH) using a Note secured by your *existing* income property (Substitution of Security).
    2.  Sell the SFH immediately for cash (break-even price).
    3.  Pay off the down payment; keep the bulk cash.
    4.  Lend the cash at a rate higher than the note on the income property.
*   **The Math:**
    *   *Cost:* $400k debt @ 6% interest ($24k/yr cost).
    *   *Revenue:* Lend $400k @ 9% + 3 Points + Fees ($36k-$40k/yr revenue).
    *   *Result:* Positive arbitrage on borrowed money.
*   **Why:** This converts "lazy equity" into "inventory" (cash) for the lending business without selling the original cash-flowing asset.

*   **Mindset:** "You are accused of a wasted life." Decisions must be made by looking at the ultimate end.
*   **Application:** When analyzing a deal or a career path, ask: "Will this contribution outlive me?" If not, it is efficiency without meaning.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Multiplex Capital Extraction
*   **Situation:** Investor owns a multiplex with $400k+ equity and $2k/mo cash flow. The equity is "trapped" and not maximizing yield.
*   **Strategy Used:**
    1.  Identify a break-even Single Family Home (SFH).
    2.  Buy SFH for $430k ($30k down, $400k note).
    3.  **Twist:** Secure the $400k note against the *Multiplex*, not the SFH.
    4.  Sell the SFH immediately for $430k (net).
*   **Outcome:**
    *   Investor walks away with $400k liquid cash.
    *   Investor incurs a $2,000/mo debt service (6%).
    *   Investor lends the $400k out at 9% + 3 points + fees.
*   **Key Lesson:** The goal wasn't to flip the house for profit; the goal was to use the transaction to extract liquidity to stock the "Hardware Store" (lending business).
