## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Oxygen Mask Protocol
* **Trigger:** IF you desire to help others (financially or emotionally).
* **Action:** THEN you must secure your own stability first.
* **Why:** "You cannot help the person next to you without first having a full set of air in your lungs... anyone gasping for air cannot help."

### Rule: The MedBed Acquisition Metric
* **Trigger:** IF evaluating a property for the medical student niche.
* **Action:** THEN ignore standard metrics; calculate Price Per Bedroom.
* **The Math:** $$ \text{Value} = \frac{\text{Acquisition Cost} + \text{Remodel Costs}}{\text{Number of Bedrooms}} $$
* **Benchmark:** Target is $\le \$60,000$ per bedroom (in secondary markets/historical context).

### Rule: The Worst-Case Safety Net (The A.B. Label Rule)
* **Trigger:** IF entering a niche market (e.g., MedBeds).
* **Action:** THEN ensure the property "pencils" (breaks even or profits) as a standard, long-term rental first.
* **Why:** If the niche market collapses, the asset must still survive as a generic rental. Everything above that is "gravy."

### Rule: The Fractional Master Lease
* **Trigger:** IF renting to multiple unrelated occupants (where zoning limits numbers).
* **Action:** THEN sign a "Master Rental Agreement" for the entire unit, and have tenants sign as "fractional members" of that master agreement.
* **Constraint:** Must verify local jurisdiction definitions of "unrelated persons" (e.g., Seattle allows 8).

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Motion vs. Stagnation (The Airport Curb)**
* **Mindset:** "Anything stagnant is in trouble. Anything that is not progressing will be taken out of the game."
* **Application:** Like a car at the airport arrival curb, you must keep moving or you will be towed. In business, constant forward motion is required to avoid obsolescence.

* **Principle:** **Major in the Major**
* **Mindset:** Put the luggage in the overhead bin first, then fit the coats around it.
* **Application:** Handle the big priorities (acquisition, financing) first. Do not let minor details (coats) crowd out the major objectives.

* **Principle:** **The Fire in the Eye**
* **Mindset:** Success requires a "passion for the pursuit inside the game itself."
* **Application:** Enthusiasm is a magnet for opportunity and investors. If you don't wake up excited to create, you are at a disadvantage against those who do.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Providence of Necessity (Francisco Franco)
* **Situation:** Ryan bought a building, lost his job 1 month later. A tenant moved out, leaving roommates unable to pay.
* **Strategy Used:** Furnished the empty unit and marketed it to the nearby university hospital for short-term rotation. Found a vascular surgeon from Spain.
* **Outcome:** Surgeon paid full month rent for 23 days. Ryan realized the demand for "MedBeds" (high rent, low wear-and-tear).
* **Key Lesson:** "The big things come out of sheer and utter necessity." Also, scarcity allows you to break standard lease norms (1-year vs. 1-month).

* **Case Name:** The Secondary Market Arbitrage
* **Situation:** Expanding the MedBed model outside of Chicago.
* **Strategy Used:** Identify markets with substantial medical infrastructure (Baltimore, San Antonio) where rent remains consistent with Chicago, but acquisition costs are lower.
* **Outcome:** Rents remain high (same student demographic), but acquisition cost drops to ~50% of Chicago prices.
* **Key Lesson:** The "customer" (medical student) pays a national standardized rate, but real estate is local. Arbitrage the difference.
