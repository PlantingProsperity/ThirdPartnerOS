## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Private Lending Pivot
*   **Trigger:** IF [Banks tighten lending criteria] AND [Public perception is "doom and gloom"].
*   **Action:** THEN [Become a Private Lender].
*   **The Logic:** Borrow funds at low cost (e.g., 5-7% via Lines of Credit/Investors) -> Lend to borrowers rejected by banks at high rates (12% + points) -> Secure 1st position on 70% LTV assets.
*   **Why:** "When banks make it difficult, private lenders just start ringing their hands together."

### Rule: The Term Hunter Acquisition (The Wrap Setup)
*   **Trigger:** IF [Intending to Flip a property] AND [Banking market is tight].
*   **Action:** THEN [Must acquire with Seller Financing (Terms)].
*   **The Math:** Buy with Owner Finance -> Sell via Wrap Note.
*   **Why:** You cannot rely on an all-cash buyer (bank dependent) to cash you out. You must provide the financing to the end buyer to move the asset.

### Rule: The Expired Listing Protocol
*   **Trigger:** IF [Commercial/Multi-family listing > 6 months (Expired)].
*   **Action:** THEN [Propose Owner Financing terms].
*   **Why:** The property likely didn't sell because buyers couldn't get a bank loan (requires 40% down). The seller is now an "open audience" for creative terms.

### Rule: The "Sandwich" Lease Option
*   **Trigger:** IF [Seller has low fixed-rate mortgage] AND [Seller must move/has cash flow problem] AND [House won't sell due to tight banks].
*   **Action:** THEN [Lease property at Seller's PITI cost] + [Sub-lease at Market Rate] + [Secure 7+ Year Option to Buy].
*   **The Math:** `Lease Payment to Seller = (Mortgage + Tax + Ins)` -> `Income = Market Rent - Payment to Seller` -> `Profit = Spread`.
*   **Why:** Solves seller's immediate cash flow crisis while controlling equity until banking markets loosen.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Law of Exposure (Newtonian Reaction)
*   **Mindset:** "For every action there's an equal and opposite reaction." The more you do, the more you will be attacked.
*   **Application:** Accept lawsuits, lies, and jealousy as "Celebrity Training." Do not recoil; dial direct.

*   **Principle:** The Unchanging Buyer
*   **Mindset:** There is no "Buyer's Market" or "Seller's Market."
*   **Application:** "We are always buyers." If you can name the price and terms, you buy. Never let market sentiment dictate your activity.

*   **Principle:** Contextual Compartmentalization
*   **Mindset:** "Exposure takes too great of a toll when you let it affect the next thing."
*   **Application:** Do not let the stress of a lawsuit or negotiation bleed into a moment with family or the next business meeting. Be where you are.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Lunch Counter "Six-Top"
*   **Situation:** Greg overheard six young entrepreneurs discussing his background ("old money") and strategies while sitting at the next table.
*   **Strategy Used:** "Dial Direct." He did not interrupt initially; he listened, then introduced himself calmly with a business card.
*   **Outcome:** Left six business cards; established dominance/authority without a fight.
*   **Key Lesson:** Confront exposure head-on. Don't hide.

*   **Case Name:** The Culvert Pipe
*   **Situation:** Greg was mentally consumed by business exposure (lawsuits/risks) while at the beach with his family. Daughter wanted to crawl through a wet pipe.
*   **Strategy Used:** Compartmentalization. He consciously "flipped a switch" to forget next week's problems and crawled through the pipe.
*   **Outcome:** Created a core memory for his daughter ("Best day of my life").
*   **Key Lesson:** Exposure wins if it steals your present joy. You must separate business war from personal peace.

*   **Case Name:** The Out-of-State Transfer
*   **Situation:** Seller moved for a job; house sitting empty. Fixed 30-year mortgage payment ~$650. Market Rent ~$1200-$1300.
*   **Strategy Used:** Lease Option (Sandwich). Greg leased it for $1,000 (covering PITI), sub-leased for profit, with a 7-year option to buy.
*   **Outcome:** Seller's cash flow problem solved; Greg controls asset and cash flow without bank financing.
*   **Key Lesson:** In tight markets, solve for the seller's monthly obligation to gain control of the equity.
