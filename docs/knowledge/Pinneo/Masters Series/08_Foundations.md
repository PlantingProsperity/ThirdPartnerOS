## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The "Romance" Assessment
* **Trigger:** IF approaching the property from the street.
* **Action:** THEN evaluate the complexity of lines.
* **Logic:** Tudors/Craftsmans (complex lines) = High Value/Romance. Split-level boxes (simple gable) = Zero Romance.
* **Why:** Romance moves the buyer's gauge; boxes do not.

### Rule: The Roof Pitch Expandability Test
* **Trigger:** IF looking at the roof pitch.
* **Action:** THEN measure visual steepness.
* **The Math:**
    *   IF Pitch = 4/12 (Low slope) -> N/A for attic expansion.
    *   IF Pitch = 12/12 (Steep slope) -> High potential for dormers/attic rooms.
* **Why:** Steep roofs shed water better (Seattle context) and offer cheap square footage expansion potential.

### Rule: The Overlay Insulation Check
* **Trigger:** IF siding is Vinyl or Aluminum Overlay.
* **Action:** THEN look for small plugged drill holes in the siding.
* **The Math:** N/A
* **Why:** Plugged holes indicate blown-in insulation. If Urea-Formaldehyde foam was used, the house is toxic.

### Rule: Garage Door Headroom
* **Trigger:** IF inspecting an older garage for door replacement.
* **Action:** THEN measure distance from top of door opening to bottom of rafters.
* **The Math:** $Height \ge 16\ inches$
* **Why:** You need 16 inches for the swing radius of a new door. If <16 inches, replacement is expensive/difficult.

### Rule: The Oil Tank "Smoking Gun"
* **Trigger:** IF furnace is Gas, BUT house is old.
* **Action:** THEN scan the concrete slab near the furnace base for copper lines.
* **The Math:** Look for 3/8" or 1/4" crimped copper tubing sticking out of concrete.
* **Why:** These lines are evidence of an abandoned (and potentially leaking) underground oil tank. Environmental liability.

### Rule: Basement Sewer Gravity Test
* **Trigger:** IF assessing basement for a potential bathroom addition.
* **Action:** THEN observe where the main sewer line exits the house.
* **The Math:**
    *   IF Line goes into slab -> Feasible.
    *   IF Line exits wall 3ft above floor -> Expensive (requires pump/ejection system).
* **Why:** Gravity is free; pumps break and cost money.

* **Mindset:** You do not have time for a full inspection (MRI) during the first walk-through. You must check the "pulse" (vital signs: roof, foundation, electrical) in minutes to determine if the patient is worth saving.
* **Application:** Scan big-ticket items (Roof tear-off, Sewer line, Electrical mast) while talking about "cats" with the seller.

* **Mindset:** "When you start becoming addicted to moments, you really start looking for moments because they are the essence of life."

* **Principle:** Control the Environment
* **Mindset:** "I can control my property. I have little to no control over my neighbor's property."
* **Application:** Never buy a house based only on itself; the neighbor's trash or deferred maintenance dictates your value.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The $9,000 Bush (Chinese Maple)
* **Situation:** Greg bought a listed property with a rare, massive Chinese Maple. Two days before closing, sellers dug it up leaving a crater, claiming sentimentality.
* **Strategy Used:** Binding mediation and arbitration at the local MLS. Demanded Escrow Holdback.
* **Outcome:** The agent spent her entire commission plus $3,000 personal funds to replace the tree with an exact match.
* **Key Lesson:** Mature landscaping is a valuable fixture. If it's missing, hold funds.

* **Case Name:** The Mahogany Inlay
* **Situation:** House had ugly yellow/mustard shag carpet.
* **Strategy Used:** "Forensic" check—opened closet doors where carpet installation often stops/gaps.
* **Outcome:** Discovered oak hardwood with dark mahogany diamond inlays underneath.
* **Key Lesson:** Sellers often cover assets with trends. Look in the closets to find the true floor.

* **Case Name:** The Leaded Glass Nightmare
* **Situation:** Bought a home with a small 6x6 inch broken pane in a leaded glass window. Thought it was a minor fix.
* **Strategy Used:** Attempted repair.
* **Outcome:** Required a specialized artist; window was painted shut; new glass didn't match the bubbles/waviness of the 80-year-old glass.
* **Key Lesson:** Vintage "Romance" (wood windows/leaded glass) is incredibly expensive to restore properly.
