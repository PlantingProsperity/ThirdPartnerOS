## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Sharpie Credit Card System
*   **Trigger:** IF incurring a business expense (Lunch, Office Supplies, Gas).
*   **Action:** THEN pay with a specific credit card dedicated *only* to that expense category, labeled on the face with a black Sharpie.
*   **The Math:** Total at bottom of Monthly Statement = Total Deduction for that Category. (No itemization required).
*   **Why:** Eliminates the need to decipher and categorize individual line items at the end of the month.

### Rule: Repair vs. Improvement Decision
*   **Trigger:** IF spending money on a property fix (e.g., new fireplaces).
*   **Action:** THEN consult CPA to classify as "Repair" or "Improvement."
*   **The Math:**
    *   *Repair:* 100% deduction in current tax year.
    *   *Improvement:* Depreciated over 27.5 years.
*   **Why:** Strategic tax planning depends on whether you need a massive write-off now or a spread-out deduction later.

### Rule: The Refinance/Acquisition Trap
*   **Trigger:** IF Buying, Selling, or Refinancing a property.
*   **Action:** THEN immediately copy the Closing Statement and place it in the *current tax year* file.
*   **The Math:** Sum(Loan Origination Fees + Doc Prep + Appraisal + Courier Fees) = Deduction.
*   **Why:** These fees are deductible in the year of the event but are often lost in permanent property files rather than tax files.

### Rule: W-2 Withholding Adjustment
*   **Trigger:** IF you have a W-2 job AND you own real estate generating tax losses.
*   **Action:** THEN adjust employer withholding downward.
*   **The Math:** $ Monthly Cash Flow Increase = (Projected Real Estate Loss × Tax Bracket) / 12.
*   **Why:** Stop lending money to the government interest-free. Increase monthly liquidity for investing.

### Rule: Private Note Interest Reporting
*   **Trigger:** IF paying a private lender (no bank statement provided).
*   **Action:** THEN generate a monthly spreadsheet tracking Principal vs. Interest.
*   **The Math:** Payment - Interest Portion = Principal Reduction.
*   **Why:** You must prove the interest deduction to the IRS; private lenders may not provide a 1098.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The 60-Year Truce (AB Label)
*   **Situation:** Newlyweds AB and Susie Label had an argument months into their marriage.
*   **Strategy Used:** Realized the hilarity and pointlessness of the conflict mid-fight. Committed to never argue again.
*   **Outcome:** 60 years of marriage without a single argument.
*   **Key Lesson:** Arguing has zero upside. It consumes energy with no production. Eliminate it.

*   **Case Name:** The 12-Card Wallet
*   **Situation:** A high-level entrepreneur paying for a business lunch.
*   **Strategy Used:** Carries 12 credit cards. Uses a black Sharpie to write the specific expense category (e.g., "Entertainment") on the face of the card.
*   **Outcome:** 100% automated accounting. The statement total equals the category total.
*   **Key Lesson:** Systematize categorization at the point of sale to eliminate backend administrative work.

*   **Case Name:** Nob Hill Accounting
*   **Situation:** Tracking expenses for a specific property.
*   **Strategy Used:** Tagging every single transaction in Quicken with "/ Nob Hill" (e.g., "Utility / Nob Hill").
*   **Outcome:** Ability to print a "Jan 1 to Dec 31" report for that specific asset instantly.
*   **Key Lesson:** Never co-mingle funds or descriptions. Specificity allows for instant P&L generation.
