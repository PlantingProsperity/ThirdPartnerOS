## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Emotional Economics** | The business philosophy that worry and "stewing" have a quantifiable financial cost because they displace productivity. | Opportunity Cost |
| **The Sissy Principle** | A philosophy of eliminating non-productive elements from life; specifically applied here to eliminating "worry" by paying to resolve issues. | Productivity |
| **Shared Spaces** | An asset class strategy involving breaking a large unit (house, office, marina) into smaller, separately rented components with common areas to increase net revenue. | Asset Allocation |
| **The Golf Ball Theory** | A metaphor for value packaging: Manufacturers sell in "trainloads" (wholesale/low margin), but retailers sell in "sleeves" (retail/high margin). Investors should buy "trainloads" (large vacant spaces) and sell "sleeves" (individual suites/rooms). | Market Positioning |
| **Bed-Meds** | A specific niche of rooming houses designed for medical interns, located within walking distance of teaching hospitals. | Niche Housing |
| **Conditional Use Permit (CUP)** | A legal mechanism to use a property for a purpose other than its zoning (e.g., business use in a residential zone), often adding significant value without physical remodeling. | Zoning/Entitlement |
| **Live-Aboards** | Tenants who use a boat as their primary residence; a specific demographic for marina "shared space" conversions. | Marina Investing |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Emotional Buy-Out (The $2,000 Check)
* **Trigger:** IF an annoyance/legal issue arises where you are "right" but fighting it consumes mental bandwidth.
* **Action:** THEN calculate the cost to "make it go away" immediately versus the cost of your time and emotional energy.
* **The Math:** `IF (Settlement Cost) < (Hours of Worry * Your Hourly Value)` -> **PAY IT.**
    * *Greg's Benchmark:* "If I were to bill for my time, it would be somewhere around a thousand dollars an hour."
* **Why:** Emotion displaces productivity. Fighting to be "right" is often less profitable than paying to be "done."

### Rule: The Rooming House Viability Test
* **Trigger:** IF analyzing a single-family home for conversion to a Shared Space rooming house.
* **Conditions:**
    1.  **Proximity:** Must be strictly *walking distance* to a major University or Economic Center (Hospital). Public transit is not sufficient.
    2.  **Basement:** Ceiling height must be code-compliant (often 7ft+) and allow for egress windows.
    3.  **Layout:** Ability to create common areas + transition areas (changing spaces) in bathrooms.
* **The Math:**
    *   *Single Family Rent:* $4,000/mo (Break-even or loss).
    *   *Shared Space Rent:* 11 Bedrooms * $500/mo = $5,500 - $6,000/mo (Positive Cash Flow).
* **Why:** You monetize the same square footage multiple times by selling "sleeves" instead of the "trainload."

### Rule: The Office Suite Split
* **Trigger:** IF you find 4,000–5,000 sq. ft. of vacant office space sitting on the market.
* **Action:** Subdivide into small suites (approx. $500–$700/mo) with shared reception, kitchenette, and conference rooms.
* **Why:** There is higher demand for small affordable footprints than large corporate leases. It doubles net revenue and diversifies vacancy risk.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Productivity > Righteousness**
* **Mindset:** "Do you want to be right, or do you want to be productive? Is this the hill you want to die on?"
* **Application:** When facing a low-stakes conflict (under $2k), do not engage in the fight. Treat the settlement as a business expense to purchase mental clarity.

* **Principle:** **The Young Entrepreneur's Sport**
* **Mindset:** Shared spaces generate massive cash flow but require intense, hands-on management (dealing with transient tenants, theft risk, interpersonal conflicts).
* **Application:** Use this strategy early in your career to build capital and leverageable equity when you have more energy than money. As you age, move to less management-intensive assets.

* **Principle:** **Monetize the Amenities**
* **Mindset:** In shared spaces, every convenience is a profit center.
* **Application:** Do not just provide laundry or water; install coin-operated machines. A shower in a marina becomes a timed revenue stream.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Slip-and-Fall Lawsuit
* **Situation:** Greg was sued for a minor injury on a property he hadn't owned for five years. He was legally innocent.
* **Strategy Used:** Emotional Economics / The Buy-Out. Instead of fighting to prove innocence (which would take hours of "stewing"), he paid the attorney $2,000 to settle it immediately.
* **Outcome:** Cost $2,000 cash, but saved estimated $7,000+ in time/energy (valued at $1,000/hr).
* **Key Lesson:** Time and energy are worth more than the cost of being "right."

* **Case Name:** The UW Rooming House
* **Situation:** A 4,500 sq. ft. house near Univ. of Washington listed for $500k. Rent as single-family would be $4k/mo (break-even).
* **Strategy Used:** Shared Space Conversion. Remodeled to 11 bedrooms by finishing basement and converting living/dining rooms.
* **Outcome:** Rents increased to ~$5,500–$6,000/mo ($500/room). Created a "cash flow machine."
* **Key Lesson:** Proximity to a university + high bedroom count = viable cash flow where single-family rental fails.

* **Case Name:** The Psychologist House
* **Situation:** Residential home adjacent to high-density zoning.
* **Strategy Used:** Conditional Use Permit (CUP). Obtained permit to rent rooms to individual psychologists with a shared waiting room.
* **Outcome:** Rental income doubled compared to residential rates without any physical remodeling.
* **Key Lesson:** Value can be created through paperwork (zoning/usage change) rather than construction.

* **Case Name:** The Lake Union Marina
* **Situation:** Marina asking price didn't pencil out based on current moorage rents.
* **Strategy Used:** Change of Use -> Live-Aboards. Installed sewer/power, built a bathhouse with coin-op showers/laundry.
* **Outcome:** Higher rents (live-aboards pay more than storage), new profit centers (laundry/showers), eventually converted to condos for massive exit.
* **Key Lesson:** Changing the tenant profile (from boat storage to housing) changes the economics.

* **Case Name:** The Sub-Lease Hustle
* **Situation:** Student with no money for down payment wanted cash flow.
* **Strategy Used:** Leased a large unit with a "right to sublease" clause. Lived in one room, rented 5 others out.
* **Outcome:** Lived rent-free with positive cash flow.
* **Key Lesson:** You don't need to own the asset to monetize the shared space concept; you just need control of the lease.
