## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Owner-User Pivot
* **Trigger:** IF [You are currently leasing or renting space to facilitate your own business or passion]
* **Action:** THEN [Figure out a way to buy the facility to facilitate that activity]
* **The Math:** `Current Rent Payment ≈ Mortgage Serviceability` (e.g., $7,000/month rent pays for ~$1.4M mortgage at 6% interest only).
* **Why:** You are the best tenant you could possibly acquire (you pay on time, you don't damage the property). Rent builds the landlord's equity; ownership builds yours.

### Rule: The Passion Syndication (The "Club" Model)
* **Trigger:** IF [A passion requires a facility too expensive for one person OR usage is sporadic/social (e.g., hunting, football games)]
* **Action:** THEN [Form a group/LLC with peers sharing the passion and charge "Annual Dues"]
* **The Math:** `Sum(Member Dues) ≥ Mortgage + Taxes + Insurance`
* **Why:** The group gains usage of the facility/land, while the "dues" pay off a free-and-clear asset over 20 years.

### Rule: The Business Mortality Check
* **Trigger:** IF [Evaluating a small business where the owner IS the brand/talent (e.g., Doctor, Boat Builder)]
* **Action:** THEN [Separate the business operation from the real estate asset]
* **The Math:** `Retirement Value = Real Estate Equity + Lease Income` (Business sale value is often nominal if the talent leaves).
* **Why:** When the owner retires, the business often dies or sells for little. The building remains and produces lease income indefinitely.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** The McDonald's Paradigm
* **Mindset:** "Burgers and fries was simply the storefront that paid for the dirt."
* **Application:** Do not view your business merely as a profit generator; view it as a tenant that buys you a portfolio of buildings.

* **Principle:** Passion as the Centerpiece
* **Mindset:** "If you’re not passionate about what you do... success will elude you."
* **Application:** Identify what you love, then strictly define the "Structure on Dirt" required to house it.

* **Principle:** The "Free and Clear" Retirement
* **Mindset:** Real estate is the "end-all asset" of a business career.
* **Application:** 35 years of business operations should result in a portfolio of paid-off buildings, not just a gold watch or a business that can't be sold without the founder.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Horse Facility (Trilogy)
* **Situation:** Greg's wife loves horses; needed a barn/arena. Owning horses is expensive.
* **Strategy Used:** Purchased a facility with 18 stalls. Kept 1 for themselves, rented 17 to boarders at $850/mo.
* **Outcome:** Boarding revenue covers hay, grain, staff, AND the mortgage, taxes, and insurance.
* **Key Lesson:** A "money-losing" hobby can pay for a massive real estate asset if you scale it to include paying customers (tenants).

* **Case Name:** Notre Dame "Party House"
* **Situation:** A group of friends spent money on hotels/parking for football games every season.
* **Strategy Used:** Formed a club, bought a small house 1.5 blocks from the stadium, paved the yard for parking.
* **Outcome:** Members pay "dues" (replacing hotel costs) which cover the mortgage. They have a private clubhouse.
* **Key Lesson:** Syndicating a passion (The Club Model) allows a group to acquire assets using money they were already spending on consumables.

* **Case Name:** The Ski Shop Tycoon
* **Situation:** Aspiring Olympian started a small ski shop; initially rented space.
* **Strategy Used:** As the business grew, he bought small buildings, then larger ones to house the shop.
* **Outcome:** Retired owning 600,000 sq ft of commercial buildings. Sold the business, but kept the buildings and collects rent from the new owners.
* **Key Lesson:** The business is temporary; the real estate portfolio built *by* the business is the permanent retirement.

* **Case Name:** The College Inn (Abie Label)
* **Situation:** Passion was helping/educating exchange students, but housing them was a logistical nightmare.
* **Strategy Used:** Purchased a hotel (The College Inn) and the pub downstairs on the UW campus.
* **Outcome:** Student rents paid off the building over 30 years; asset is now free and clear.
* **Key Lesson:** Real estate can be a secondary pursuit driven by a logistical need of the primary passion.

* **Case Name:** The Wine Shop
* **Situation:** Couple loved wine/collecting; wanted to open a shop.
* **Strategy Used:** Instead of leasing retail space, purchased a commercial building.
* **Outcome:** Retail profits pay employee overhead + Mortgage/Taxes/Insurance on the building.
* **Key Lesson:** Even niche retail passions should be Owner-Users to secure long-term equity.
