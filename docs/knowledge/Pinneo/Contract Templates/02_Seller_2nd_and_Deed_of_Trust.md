## 1. THE PINNEO DICTIONARY (Domain Lexicon)

| Term | Definition (In Pinneo Context) | Related Concept |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Template 2** | An acquisition strategy for high-equity properties where a "New First" loan funds the down payment/rehab, and the seller holds the balance in a "Second" position. | Zero Down Investing |
| **New First Position Loan** | A loan originated by the buyer from a third party (bank/private) to generate cash at closing. Used to clear old debt and fund the down payment. | Leverage |
| **Prime Time Slot** | A first lien position with a very low Loan-to-Value (e.g., 50%), making it highly secure and attractive to private lenders at lower interest rates. | Hard Money vs. Private Money |
| **Substitution of Security** | A clause in a promissory note allowing the borrower to move the mortgage/collateral to a different property, preventing a forced payoff upon resale. | Portfolio Management |
| **Two Installments** | A negotiation reframe used when a seller demands "all cash," defining the "all cash" as being paid in two distinct timelines (Closing + Note Maturity). | Negotiation Scripts |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The High-Equity Filter
* **Trigger:** IF [Evaluating a property for Template 2 acquisition]
* **Action:** THEN [Verify the seller owes 50% or less of the property's value.]
* **Why:** If the underlying debt is too high (e.g., 80%), there isn't enough room to originate a "New First" loan that covers the debt and provides cash without over-leveraging the asset.

### Rule: The "New First" Calculation
* **Trigger:** IF [Structuring the cash loan for closing]
* **Action:** THEN [Borrow a maximum of 60-65% of the property value.]
* **The Math:** $$Loan_{NewFirst} \le 0.65 \times Value_{Property}$$
* **Why:** Keeping the LTV low ensures the loan remains "Prime Time" inventory (safe/secure), allowing you to borrow at 6-7% rather than hard money rates (12%+).

### Rule: The "All Cash" Rebuttal
* **Trigger:** IF [Seller says "I want all cash for this property"]
* **Action:** THEN [Reply: "I'd like to give you all cash, but in two installments. Would that work for you?"]
* **Why:** It reframes the carryback note as simply a delayed cash payment, keeping the negotiation alive.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** Constructed Leverage / Levitating the Down Payment
* **Mindset:** "The amateur asks, 'How much money do I have in the bank?' The professional asks, 'How much equity is in the deal?'"
* **Application:** Do not use personal savings for down payments. Use the gap between the seller's existing low debt and the property value to "create" the down payment via a New First loan.

* **Principle:** The Prime Time Slot
* **Mindset:** Lenders are driven by security, not just yield.
* **Application:** By offering a lender a First Position lien at only 50% LTV, you are offering them safety. You can dictate lower interest rates (6-7%) because you are essentially "doing them a favor" by providing a secure place for their capital.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The $100k Fixer (Template 2)
* **Situation:** Property worth $100k. Seller owes $30k. Seller needs walking money. Property needs $10k rehab. Buyer has $0 cash.
* **Strategy Used:** Template 2 (New First + Seller Second).
    *   Buyer borrows **$50,000 New First Loan** (Private Lender).
    *   **Distribution of $50k:** $30k pays off old debt; $10k cash to Seller; $10k cash to Buyer (for rehab).
    *   **Seller Consideration:** Seller receives $10k cash + **$60k Note** (2nd Deed of Trust).
* **Outcome:**
    *   **Buyer:** Owns property, has $10k for repairs, $0 personal money used. Total debt: $110k (over-leveraged against purchase price, but funded).
    *   **Seller:** Debt free, $10k cash in pocket, holds interest-bearing note for $60k.
    *   **Lender:** Safe at 50% LTV.
* **Key Lesson:** You can fund the purchase, the down payment to the seller, and the rehab costs entirely by leveraging the property's equity, provided the seller's existing debt is low.
