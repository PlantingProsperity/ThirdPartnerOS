| **Sheetrock In** | The typical boundary definition for a condo unit. The owner owns the air space and finish from the paint/drywall inward. | Surveying |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Gourley Profit Formula (Conversion Viability)
*   **Trigger:** IF assessing an apartment building for potential conversion to condos.
*   **The Math:**
    $$ \text{Retail Value} - (13\% \text{ Load}) - \text{Hard Costs} - \text{Soft Costs} = \text{Net Profit} $$
    *   *Variables defined in text:*
        *   **Retail Value:** Comparative market value of individual units.
        *   **13% Load:** Covers Commissions, Escrow, Title, and Marketing.
        *   **Hard Costs:** Renovation (Example used: ~$22,000/door).
        *   **Soft Costs:** Interest carry/Holding costs (Example used: ~$14,000/door).
*   **The Decision:** IF Net Profit < $20,000 per door, THEN **Walk Away**. (Craig's personal minimum).

### Rule: The "Cash for Keys" Arbitrage
*   **Trigger:** IF you need a tenant out early for renovation, AND holding costs are high.
*   **Action:** Offer 100% deposit refund + No cleaning required + Cash Bonus ($500–$2,000).
*   **Why:** "If you're not going to purchase a unit... I'll make it worth your while to move early." The cash payout is often cheaper than the daily interest carry of a stalled project.

### Rule: The Pre-Close Entitlement (Speed)
*   **Trigger:** IF the building is under contract (Escrow) but not yet closed.
*   **Action:** Negotiate access for surveyors, engineers, and legal drafting during the escrow period.
*   **Result:** Have the Public Offering Statement and Sales team ready within 24 hours of closing.
*   **Why:** "The clock of soft costs are not ticking... and yet you are efficiently moving through your game plan."

### Rule: The "Touch It, Warrant It" Liability
*   **Trigger:** IF you are converting a building in a litigious jurisdiction (e.g., WA).
*   **Action:** Be aware that any system you physically alter (plumbing, electrical) carries a statutory warranty (e.g., 4 years).
*   **Strategy:** Sell "As-Is" to avoid triggering new warranties, OR ensure reserves are high and insurance is a "Wrap Policy."

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** **Value Arbitrage via Definition**
*   **Mindset:** "This building is now valued based on a comparative market condominium approach... versus an apartment building being valued at an income approach."
*   **Application:** Realize that changing the *paper* definition of a property changes the *math* used to value it. You profit from the gap between Cap Rate valuation and Retail Comp valuation.

*   **Principle:** **Attention is the Variable**
*   **Mindset:** "It's my lack of attention that is my stumbling block. It's not that the answers aren't there."
*   **Application:** When a deal looks dead or a problem insolvable, assume you are missing a component (inattention) rather than facing an accident of fate.

*   **Principle:** **Form Over Structure**
*   **Mindset:** "Condominium is merely a form of ownership."
*   **Application:** Stop looking for "Condo buildings." Look for storage units, parking lots, or moorage facilities. Anything with boundaries can be "condominium-ized" to create equity.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Parking Lot Arbitrage
*   **Situation:** A 4-unit building with 16 parking spaces in a high-density area.
*   **Strategy Used:** Converted to condos. Sold the 4 living units, but treated the extra 12 parking spaces as sellable commodities (likely via Limited Common Elements or separate titles).
*   **Outcome:** The net sales gain of the parking exceeded the value of the 4 units.
*   **Key Lesson:** Don't just look at the building; look at the scarcity of the ancillary assets (parking).

*   **Case Name:** The "Notice Party"
*   **Situation:** Closing on a multi-building project; needed to serve legal notices to tenants.
*   **Strategy Used:** Instead of mailing notices, hosted a dinner with wine. Invited the lender. Handed out the "Notice of Termination," "Public Offering Statement," and "Listing Package" simultaneously.
*   **Outcome:** Sold three units that night.
*   **Key Lesson:** Turn a negative legal requirement (eviction notice) into an exclusive sales event.

*   **Case Name:** The 12-Building Shuffle
*   **Situation:** Converting a large 12-building complex; wanted to maintain cash flow during construction.
*   **Strategy Used:** Paid tenants in front buildings to move to the back buildings.
*   **Outcome:** Freed up front inventory for construction while keeping rental income in the rear.
*   **Key Lesson:** Use "Cash for Keys" to control logistics and cash flow simultaneously.
