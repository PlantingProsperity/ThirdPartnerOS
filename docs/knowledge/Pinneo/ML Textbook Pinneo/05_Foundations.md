## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Daily Launch (10 Questions)
* **Trigger:** Daily morning routine.
* **Action:** Write 10 "Questions of Proposition" aimed at moving you toward your specific goal (e.g., funding a fourplex). Make the 10 calls before the day ends.
* **Why:** "The answer to every question you do not ask is no."

### Rule: The Investor Pitch (The "If I Were" Algorithm)
* **Trigger:** Approaching a contact (friend/family) for capital.
* **Action:** Use the conditional close.
* **The Script Logic:** IF [I locate a property] AND [Collateralize it with equity/cash flow] THEN [Would you lend at X%?]
* **Why:** It removes the pressure of a specific deal initially and focuses on the concept of the relationship and return.

### Rule: The "Summit Meeting" Upsell
* **Trigger:** 6-12 months of perfect payment history with a Seller Carry/Private Lender.
* **Action:** Schedule a meeting, review the perfection of the current note, then ask about the underperformance of their *other* investments.
* **The Math:** Compare the consistent 6% (or agreed rate) they get from you vs. the volatility of their stock market portfolio.
* **Why:** To transfer the rest of their portfolio into your management (borrowing more money).

### Rule: The 80/10/10 Financing Structure
* **Trigger:** Acquiring a small multiplex ($400k example).
* **Action:** Structure the capital stack to minimize personal cash.
* **The Math:**
    *   **Total Price:** $400,000
    *   **Bank Loan (1st Position):** $320,000 (80%)
    *   **Seller Note (2nd Position):** $40,000 (10%)
    *   **Down Payment (Equity/LOC):** $40,000 (10%)
*   **Cash Flow Analysis:**
    *   Bank Pmt: ~$2,000/mo
    *   Seller Pmt (6% IO): ~$200/mo
    *   LOC Pmt: ~$200/mo
    *   Operating Exp: ~$1,100/mo
    *   **Total Out:** ~$3,500/mo
    *   **Total In:** $3,100 (Rents) + $200 (Rent Raise) + $200 (Tax Savings via Depreciation) = ~$3,500/mo.
*   **Result:** Breakeven cash flow, but acquisition of a $400k asset with $0 personal cash used (if LOC is used for down payment).

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** "Encourage Your Courage"
*   **Mindset:** When you cut off the retreat (board up the back door), the universe (Providence) rewards the commitment. "I will be standing at the end of the day."
*   **Application:** Do not dabble. If you want a fourplex, your only focus is that fourplex.

*   **Principle:** Brick by Brick vs. Instant Gratification
*   **Mindset:** "Success is about knowing what you want and deciding that you are willing, brick by brick, to work hard."
*   **Application:** Reject the "lottery" mentality. Embrace the sweat and struggle of the arena.

*   **Principle:** Money Follows Management
*   **Mindset:** People lend to people, not just buildings. They lend to trustworthiness, track record, and the ability to repay.
*   **Application:** Your credit, your punctuality, and your cleanliness on a job site are your resume.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** Pitching a known contact (The "Warm Market" Ask)
> **Verbatim Script:** "I am looking to create an investment pool of dollars to buy well-located, romantic real estate. And if I were to locate a piece of property and were able to collateralize your invested dollar with that property, whereby there's plenty of equity and there's plenty of cash flow to support the amount of principal and the amount of payment that you're willing to lend me, would you be interested in lending me some money at let's say a 5 or 6% rate of return?"
> **Tone:** Professional, hypothetical, low-pressure.

> **Scenario:** The "Summit Meeting" Upsell (Turning a Seller into a larger Investor)
> **Verbatim Script:** "Hank, I just have a question for you. Is the rest of your investment portfolio performing as well as this particular piece of your investment? Are you getting 6% on a consistent, absolute count-on-it basis from the rest of your portfolio? ... Hank, if I were to be able to give you 6% on all of this part of your portfolio that's not performing to that level, and if I were able to secure that well... would you be interested in transferring your portfolio from here to there?"
> **Tone:** Consultative, confident (based on track record).

> **Scenario:** Negotiating Terms with Earl (The Reluctant Seller/Lender)

> *Greg:* [Exhales loudly] "Oh, wow. $2,000 down?"

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** Earl & Elvira
*   **Situation:** Greg wanted to buy a crummy house on a multi-family zoned lot from neighbors Earl (80s) and Elvira.
*   **Strategy Used:** Relationship building (coffee for years). When Earl wanted to sell for $60,000, Greg negotiated terms where Earl funded the deal himself.
*   **Structure:**
    *   Price: $60,000.
    *   Down Payment: $2,000 (Lent by Earl @ 6%).
    *   Balance: $58,000 (Carried by Earl @ 5%).
    *   Collateral: Substituted to a different property to allow demolition of Earl's house later.
*   **Outcome:** Acquired development land with $0 out of pocket. Earl & Elvira became major investors for a decade.
*   **Key Lesson:** "The guy that you think doesn't have a lot of money, that's the guy that has a lot of money."

*   **Case Name:** The Seattle Six-Plex
*   **Situation:** Acquiring a $750,000 building.
*   **Strategy Used:** 80/20 leverage with Seller Financing.
*   **Structure:**
    *   $150,000 Down Payment (Borrowed from unsecured bank line of credit).
    *   $600,000 Seller Note @ 6%.
*   **Outcome:** Three months later, the seller offered an additional $400k-$500k in funding because they were tired of stock market losses.
*   **Key Lesson:** Performance on the initial note unlocks the lender's remaining portfolio.
