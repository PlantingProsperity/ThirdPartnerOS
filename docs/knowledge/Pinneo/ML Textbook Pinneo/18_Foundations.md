## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The 5-Category Time Management Stack
* **Trigger:** Daily Schedule Planning.
* **Hierarchy:**
    1.  **You:** (Fill the reservoir).
    2.  **Personal World:** (Spouse, Kids, Inner Circle).
    3.  **Product:** (Hunting for real estate).
    4.  **Money:** (Hunting for funding).
    5.  **Buyers/Assigns:** (Hunting for the exit).
* **The Math:** $Time_{Admin} \in \{t < 6:00 \lor t > 22:00\}$ (Admin is done outside communicative hours).
* **Why:** You cannot give to the business if you are empty. Business tasks must be prioritized by revenue generation (Product drives Money).

### Rule: The Deputization Protocol
* **Trigger:** Encountering service workers or locals in a target investment zone.
* **Action:** Engage, explain the mission, and offer a specific financial reward for leads.
* **The Math:** $Referral Fee = 2.5\% \text{ to } 3\% \text{ of Purchase Price}$ (e.g., $15,000 on a $500k deal).
* **Why:** Scales lead generation beyond what one person can physically hunt.

### Rule: The Idle Funds Logic
* **Trigger:** Need for Private Money to fund a deal.
* **Action:** Ask contacts if they have underperforming funds, rather than asking for a loan.
* **The Math:** $Private Rate (6\%) > Bank Rate (3.5\%)$.
* **Why:** You are solving *their* problem (low yield) rather than asking for a favor.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Growth is Not Assumed**
* **Mindset:** "People don't get smaller. They have to get bigger." However, for adults, growth is not a foregone conclusion; it is a deliberate choice to walk into pain/unknowns.
* **Application:** Intentionally place yourself in uncomfortable business situations to force expansion.

* **Principle:** **Product Drives Money**
* **Mindset:** Never look for money for a hypothetical deal. Secure the "Product" (Real Estate) first.
* **Application:** It is easier to collateralize a specific building than an idea. A tangible asset converts a "no" into a "yes."

* **Principle:** **Structure Without Filling**
* **Mindset:** Administrative efficiency (databases, tech) without the core "meat" of the business (talking to people about Product, Money, Buyers) is useless.
* **Application:** Do not confuse "getting ready to play" with "playing the game."

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** Deputizing a stranger (The Barista Script) to find off-market deals.
> **Verbatim Script:** "Rhonda, do you have like two minutes that I could tell you what I do, so that if you ever hear about something that I'm looking for, it could be a very profitable day? Matter of fact, you might get paid more than you make in three months here... If I end up buying that property, Rhonda, you will get paid two and a half or three percent of the sales price... If I spend $500,000 on a small multiplex... you're going to get a check for somewhere around $15,000."
> **Tone:** Intriguing, conspiratorial, lucrative.

> **Scenario:** Asking a contact for Private Money (The Idle Funds Inquiry).
> **Verbatim Script:** "Bill, do you have funds in any kind of investment account that are currently not giving you a 6% fixed return?"
> **Tone:** Direct. **CRITICAL:** Ask the question, then be quiet. Wait for the answer.

> **Scenario:** The 5-Year-Old's Logic (Internal Monologue).
> **Verbatim Script:** "Daddy, it has to be this way. People don't get smaller. They have to get bigger. It has to be this way."
> **Tone:** Innocent but profoundly truthful.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The $400,000 Phone Call
* **Situation:** Student found a property. Seller demanded all cash. Student needed $74,000 but the property was not bankable.
* **Strategy Used:** **The Idle Funds Inquiry.** Student spent half a day calling contacts asking if they had funds earning less than 6%.
* **Outcome:** Raised $400,000 in private capital (one investor with $375k, one with $25k) before lunch.
* **Key Lesson:** Product drives money. When you have a specific deal to secure the funds against, money is abundant.

* **Case Name:** Washing the Land Cruiser
* **Situation:** Greg needs to "fill the reservoir" in under 30 minutes.
* **Strategy Used:** **The Alpha and Omega.** Washing a car provides immediate visual feedback (dirty to clean) and organizes mind space.
* **Outcome:** Reservoir filled; ready to be a "non-leaner" for the family.
* **Key Lesson:** Self-care activities don't need to be complex; they need to provide a sense of completion and satisfaction.
