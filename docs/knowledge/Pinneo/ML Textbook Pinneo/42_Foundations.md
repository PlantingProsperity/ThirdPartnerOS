## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Good is the Enemy of Great**
* **Mindset:** "Every action or endeavor is only as good as what you could be doing."
* **Application:** Continually audit if your current effort is yielding the *greatest* possible return, or if you are settling for merely "good."

* **Principle:** **Environment Dictates Possibility**
* **Mindset:** "Your very definition of possibility starts with the environment you find yourself in."
* **Application:** Curate your office, home, and views ruthlessly. If you cannot control your environment (like in prison), you lose leverage on your own output.

* **Principle:** **Obligation vs. Choice**
* **Mindset:** "I would never do something out of obligation... I want to choose my actions."
* **Application:** Reframe necessary tasks as choices. If you cannot frame it as a choice or passion, stop doing it.

* **Principle:** **The Essentialist Approach to Equity**
* **Mindset:** "The effort to produce a twenty-unit is the same effort that it took to produce a two-unit."
* **Application:** Move to larger projects sooner. Do not linger in small deals thinking they are safer; the effort is identical, only the yield is smaller.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Care Bear Negotiation
* **Situation:** Greg and Shauna needed to remove two cats (Molly and Dozer) from the house due to pregnancy risks. Daughter Everest was attached to them.
* **Strategy Used:** Shauna offered a trade: The cats leave, Everest gets any Care Bear she wants.
* **Outcome:** Everest accepted the Care Bear (approx. $40 value).
* **Key Lesson:** **Understand Your Leverage.** Greg would have paid "millions" or bought a "Care Bear franchise" to remove the cats. Everest settled for $40 because she did not understand the counterparty's desperation.

* **Case Name:** The Board Meeting Raise
* **Situation:** Greg sat on a board where 15 successful adults debated a seven-cent-an-hour raise for a cook for over an hour.
* **Strategy Used:** Evaluation of ROI on time vs. money.
* **Outcome:** Greg realized if everyone pitched in $20, the raise would be covered for a year. He resigned; it was his last board meeting.
* **Key Lesson:** Avoid committees and boards. They are a "waste of freaking time." Fly solo whenever possible.

* **Case Name:** The Utility Trailer vs. The Backhoe
* **Situation:** For 30 years, Greg hand-unloaded utility trailers at the dump to save money/effort.
* **Strategy Used:** Capital Investment for Efficiency (Buying a backhoe/hydraulic lift).
* **Outcome:** Replaced back-breaking labor with "pushing a button."
* **Key Lesson:** Buy the tool sooner. Don't trade your body for money.
