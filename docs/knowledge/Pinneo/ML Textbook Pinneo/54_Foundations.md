## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Decoy (Salting the Box)
* **Trigger:** IF starting the day (or a capital raise) with an empty container.
* **Action:** THEN place a small amount of money (seed capital) inside visible to others.
* **The Math:** N/A
* **Why:** An empty box suggests failure or that the person doesn't actually need help. A box with money acts as a "magnet" (Social Proof).

### Rule: The Law of Averages (Traffic Sorting)
* **Trigger:** IF rejected by a passerby.
* **Action:** THEN remain indifferent ("It's all good") and wait for the next person.
* **The Math:** 20 passersby = 5 contributors (25% conversion rate).
* **Why:** Rejection is not personal; it is simply data sorting. You must endure the 15 "no's" to get the 5 "yes's".

### Rule: Territory Defense (The Bear Strategy)
* **Trigger:** IF a competitor is standing in your established spot (6th and Pine).
* **Action:** THEN walk toward the spot confidently.
* **The Math:** N/A
* **Why:** Tenure creates ownership. Because Otis is consistent ("The Bear"), competitors leave voluntarily without confrontation upon seeing him.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** "Just Be Otis" (Identity as Service)
* **Mindset:** "If I'm out there cussing... that's bad for me. So hey, I just be Otis. As long as I’m Otis, it’s all good."
* **Application:** Authenticity is a business asset. Consistency of character (impervious to rude customers) builds a brand that generates reliable revenue.

* **Principle:** The Un-Asking (Indifference)
* **Mindset:** "I ain't got to ask people... If they're going to give, they're going to give. If they don’t, it's still good. I can take it or leave it."
* **Application:** Detachment from the outcome increases the attractiveness of the offer. Desperation repels; confidence/indifference attracts.

* **Principle:** Windfall Equity
* **Mindset:** You cannot plan for the "home run" (the $1,000 bill), but you must be "on the corner" to receive it when it happens.
* **Application:** Consistency places you in the path of luck.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The $1,000 Drop
* **Situation:** Otis was standing on his corner during the holidays, not expecting anything unusual.
* **Strategy Used:** Consistency/Presence (Windfall Equity).
* **Outcome:** A passerby dropped a $1,000 bill into the box without a word.
* **Key Lesson:** You don't wake up expecting a grand slam, but if you aren't in the game (on the corner), you can never hit one.

* **Case Name:** The 8-Year Conversion
* **Situation:** A woman walked past Otis for 8 years without ever giving money.
* **Strategy Used:** Extreme Consistency. Otis remained the constant variable while her life/mindset changed.
* **Outcome:** She finally stopped and gave $1, citing "change" in the new year.
* **Key Lesson:** A "No" is only a "No" for right now. If you maintain your position, market conditions (or personal situations) will eventually shift non-buyers into buyers.

* **Case Name:** The Rude Lady (Brand Damage)
* **Situation:** Otis, while drunk years ago, cussed out a woman who didn't give money.
* **Strategy Used:** (Negative Example) Emotional reaction/Retaliation.
* **Outcome:** She returned years later; Otis apologized, but she said she would "never forget that."
* **Key Lesson:** A bad reputation (brand damage) lasts longer than a good one. Consistency in attitude is required for long-term survival.
