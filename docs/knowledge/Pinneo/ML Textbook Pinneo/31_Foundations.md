## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Phone Protocol (Confidence Builder)
*   **Trigger:** IF the phone rings and you choose to answer it.
*   **Action:** THEN state: "Good afternoon, this is [Name]. You have my full attention. How can I help you?"
*   **Why:** In a multitasking world, 100% focus shocks the recipient and instantly establishes authority and confidence.

### Rule: The Out-of-Area Promoter Interrogation
*   **Trigger:** IF a vendor/promoter pitches an investment outside your local area.
*   **Action:** THEN ask these three questions before proceeding:
    1. "If I act on your advice, are you getting paid?"
    2. "Do you live here? What is your personal longevity in this location?"
    3. "Do you have your own money in this investment (not commission proceeds, but personal funds signed on the loan)?"
*   **Why:** To determine if they are a "Pilot" (sharing the risk) or standing on the shoreline in a survival suit telling you to swim.

### Rule: The "3-Cap" Liquidation Trigger
*   **Trigger:** IF the local market enters a frenzy where inferior properties sell at historically low Cap Rates (e.g., 3%).
*   **Action:** THEN list "middle-row" holding properties immediately to capture the irrational premium.
*   **The Math:** $Value = \frac{NOI}{Cap Rate}$. As Cap Rate $\downarrow$, Value $\uparrow$. (Selling at a 3-Cap yields a sales price significantly higher than selling at a 5-Cap).
*   **Why:** These are "subtle winds" that open and close quickly (3-4 month window).

### Rule: The Pressure Test
*   **Trigger:** IF a seller or promoter says, "You've got to write a check right now or the deal goes away."
*   **Action:** THEN let the deal go away.
*   **Why:** Due diligence cannot be bypassed. Pressure is a tactic to obscure risk.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Binary Commitment (The Pool)
*   **Mindset:** "You're either in or you're out. You are either over your head in the deep end of the pool, or stay out of the pool."
*   **Application:** Eliminate "I'll try," "We'll see," or "I'll run it up the flagpole." Make absolute commitments or none at all.

*   **Principle:** Geometry of Focus (Passenger vs. Pilot)
*   **Mindset:** You cannot rely on a pilot who isn't on the plane. If the venture fails, the person guiding you must suffer the same consequences you do.
*   **Application:** Never invest with promoters who earn upfront fees but carry no long-term liability or equity risk.

*   **Principle:** Geography of Responsibility
*   **Mindset:** The distance between you and your collateral is directly proportional to risk.
*   **Application:** If you cannot drive by your property every other day to check for "subtle winds," you are speculating, not investing, unless you have a partner who lives there and shares your ethics.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** Mr. Wizard & The Pre-Construction Condo
*   **Situation:** A student bought an out-of-state pre-construction unit based on a seminar pitch.
*   **Strategy Used:** "Passenger" strategy (trusted the vendor blindly).
*   **Outcome:** Project finished 6 months late; final value was 40% below projection; negative cash flow if rented; $40k loss if sold.
*   **Key Lesson:** Who had a loaded gun to your head? Never buy based on a prospectus without independent verification and site visits.

*   **Case Name:** The Seattle 3-Cap Surge
*   **Situation:** A localized buying frenzy occurred in a 5-block radius of Seattle; cap rates dropped to 3% (prices skyrocketed).
*   **Strategy Used:** "Owner's Presence." Greg noticed the anomaly because he drove his properties every other day. He listed two "middle-row" 4-plexes.
*   **Outcome:** Sold both at a 3-Cap (huge premium). Market corrected to 4.7-Cap one month later.
*   **Key Lesson:** You cannot see "subtle winds of opportunity" (short-term market bubbles) if you are an absentee owner living in another state.

*   **Case Name:** The Cabo/Austin/Georgia Booths
*   **Situation:** Real Estate Group meeting with vendors pitching out-of-state "turnkey" deals.
*   **Strategy Used:** Observation of the "Herd Mentality."
*   **Outcome:** People wrote checks for property they had never seen based on 5-minute pitches.
*   **Key Lesson:** Investors behave like herding animals seeking safety in numbers, but they are actually walking into a trap of low control and high reliance on others.
