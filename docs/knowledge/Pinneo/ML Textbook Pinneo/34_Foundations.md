Distinct from the corporate "Ready, Aim, Fire." | Risk Mitigation |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The "Ready, Fire, Aim" Trigger
*   **Trigger:** IF [Acquisition Price + Terms] < [Worst-Case Scenario Value (WCS)].
*   **Action:** THEN **Fire** (Execute Purchase & Sale Agreement immediately, no contingencies, non-refundable earnest money).
*   **The Math:** $WCS = (\text{Market Value to Wholesaler/Builder}) - (\text{Transaction Costs})$
*   **Why:** If the deal pencils at the worst possible outcome (e.g., flipping the contract), all other strategies (developing, holding) represent pure upside. Confidence comes from knowing the floor, not predicting the ceiling.

### Rule: Net Scraped Value (Dirt Value)
*   **Trigger:** IF evaluating a property with a low-value structure on high-density zoning.
*   **Action:** Calculate what a builder would pay for the raw land.
*   **The Math:**
    $$V_{dirt} = (P_{unit} \times N_{units}) - (C_{demo} + C_{permits})$$
    *   $V_{dirt}$: Value of the site to a builder.
    *   $P_{unit}$: Price builders pay per "door" or dwelling unit in that specific zone.
    *   $N_{units}$: Number of allowable units under zoning.
    *   $C_{demo}$: Cost to demolish existing structures.
    *   $C_{permits}$: Cost of engineering/permitting.
*   **Why:** This establishes the "floor" value. If the seller's asking price for the house is below $V_{dirt}$, the deal is safe.

### Rule: The Four-Plan Aim
*   **Trigger:** AFTER "Firing" (securing the contract).
*   **Action:** Evaluate four exit strategies to maximize profit ("The Kaleidoscope Turn").
    1.  **Plan A (The Floor):** Assign the contract to a builder/investor for a quick fee.
    2.  **Plan B:** Partner with a capital investor (they put up 100% money, you get 50% equity).
    3.  **Plan C:** Develop the land yourself (e.g., blade and build).
    4.  **Plan D:** Short plat or modify current structures (e.g., add units in back).

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The "Tight Spot" Catalyst.
*   **Mindset:** Sellers do not sell based on market value analysis; they sell to solve a problem. When a "Slow Dance" partner hits a financial wall (the "tight spot"), liquidity is more valuable to them than maximum equity.
*   **Application:** Identify the exact cash amount needed to solve the seller's problem (e.g., pay off debts + closing costs). If that number allows for a profit spread, offer exactly that number as a "solution," not a "price."

*   **Principle:** The 6 P's (Attributed to Steve Scott).
*   **Mindset:** "Prior Preparation Prevents Piss-Poor Performance."
*   **Application:** You cannot "Fire" effectively if you aren't "Ready." Readiness (knowing values, having the Pony Show, knowing builders) must precede the opportunity.

*   **Principle:** Confidence via Action.
*   **Mindset:** "We gain confidence by successful action. If we never take action, we never gain confidence."
*   **Application:** Analysis paralysis is a self-esteem issue, not an intellectual one. To cure it, execute on a "Worst-Case Scenario" safe deal.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The Mossy Land Cruiser (FJ40)
*   **Situation:** Greg spotted a 1983 Toyota Land Cruiser parked in tall grass/moss while leaving a real estate appointment. Seller hadn't driven it in 5 years; wife wanted it gone.
*   **Strategy Used:** **Eyes of Opportunity** + **Knowledge of Value**. Greg recognized specific features (parking brake location, choke light, steering wheel) identifying it as a rare 1983 model worth high value.
*   **Outcome:** Bought for $2,000 cash on the spot. Cleaned it up (pressure washed moss). Sold 3 months later for $14,000.
*   **Key Lesson:** "Readiness" (knowing the niche details of the asset) allows for instant "Firing" (buying without fear).

*   **Case Name:** The Neighbor's "Tight Spot" Package
*   **Situation:** A neighbor Greg had "Slow Danced" with for 5 years faced a financial crisis. Needed cash to cover debts. Owned a duplex on a 7-unit zoned lot and a high-end personal residence.
*   **Strategy Used:** **Ready, Fire, Aim**. Greg calculated the **WCS** for both properties.
    *   Duplex Site WCS: $1.35M (Dirt value to builder).
    *   House WCS: $625k.
    *   Total Value: ~$1.975M.
    *   Greg offered: $1.45M (The exact amount seller needed to clear debt).
*   **Outcome:** Greg secured a $500k equity spread immediately by solving the seller's urgency with an unconditional cash offer.
*   **Key Lesson:** Establish the "Floor" (WCS). If the purchase price is below the floor, buy immediately. Decide the exit strategy (Assign, Partner, Build) later.

*   **Case Name:** The Newspaper "Game"
*   **Situation:** Four friends competed to turn $10k into maximum cash in 1 year using no leverage (100% cash trading).
*   **Strategy Used:** High-velocity trading of demand items. The winner did 77 transactions.
*   **Outcome:** Winner turned $10k into $255k. Loser turned $10k into $195k (and had to pay 30% to the winner).
*   **Key Lesson:** Velocity of money and knowing the "buy" and "sell" price instantly (market knowledge) beats slow appreciation.
