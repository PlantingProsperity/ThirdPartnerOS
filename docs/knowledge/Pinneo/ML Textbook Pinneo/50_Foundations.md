## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: Construction Loan Sizing (The "Lesser Of" Governor)
* **Trigger:** IF applying for a construction loan (Single Family or Commercial).
* **The Math:**
    *   *Single Family:* Loan = Lesser of (90% of Cost) OR (80% of Value).
    *   *Commercial/Apartment:* Loan = Lesser of (80% of Cost) OR (70% of Value).
* **Why:** Lenders require "skin in the game." The bigger the project, the more skin required.

### Rule: The Signature Strength Compensation Model
* **Trigger:** IF you lack liquidity/experience and bring in a partner for "Signature Strength" (Guarantee).
* **Action:** THEN you must compensate the signer via "Preferred Return" + "Equity Participation."
* **The Math:**
    *   *Signer Gets:* Preferred Return (Interest rate > Bank rate) on any cash deployed.
    *   *Signer Gets:* 25% to 50% of Project Profits (Equity).
* **Why:** The signer is providing a Full Recourse guarantee, putting their personal assets at risk.

### Rule: The Private Lender vs. Bank "Second Position" Logic
* **Trigger:** IF structuring a deal with a 1st Position Lender and a Seller Carry-back 2nd.
* **Condition A (Bank/Conforming):** IF Lender is a Bank -> Response is **Negative**. (They fear high Total Loan-to-Value).
* **Condition B (Private/Smart Money):** IF Lender is Private -> Response is **Positive**.
* **Why (Condition B):** The Private lender sees the Seller in 2nd position as an insurance policy. If the borrower fails, the Seller *must* cure the 1st loan to avoid being wiped out.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **The Umbrella of Money**
* **Mindset:** "Money" is not one thing. It is an umbrella covering 15+ sub-specialties (Securities, Conforming, Private, Hard Money, Construction, etc.).
* **Application:** Do not expect a generalist mortgage broker to handle an entrepreneurial acquisition. You need a specialist who tracks the specific "weather" of the niche you are in.

* **Principle:** **Common Sense is a Fallacy in Lending**
* **Mindset:** Do not apply logic (e.g., "I'm putting 50% down, surely they will lend") to institutional lending.
* **Application:** A 23-year-old teacher with a W2 can get a loan easier than a self-employed millionaire. Do not fight the system with logic; navigate it with "packaging."

* **Principle:** **The "One Shot" Packaging Rule**
* **Mindset:** Lenders do not give second chances. If you submit a "Shoebox" and get a no, you cannot easily re-submit with a partner later.
* **Application:** Never submit a file until it is perfectly packaged. If you lack experience, bring the experienced partner onto the application *before* the first submission.

* **Case Name:** The 6-Unit Apartment Purchase
* **Situation:** GP found a 6-unit building on an 8-unit site. Price $1,150,000. GP wanted minimal cash down ($50k).
* **Strategy Used:** **Seller Financing via Tax Deferral.** GP negotiated with the seller's financial planner. The seller had a "zero basis" (property fully depreciated) and faced massive Capital Gains tax if they cashed out.
* **The Deal Structure:**
    *   Price: $1.15M
    *   Down: $50,000 (~4%)
    *   Loan: Seller carries $1.1M First Position Note.
    *   Terms: 5.5% Interest Only, 20-Year Balloon.
* **Outcome:** Seller defers capital gains indefinitely (only pays on principal received). GP controls $1.15M asset for $50k down with no bank qualification.
* **Key Lesson:** Solve the seller's tax problem to secure terms a bank would never offer.
