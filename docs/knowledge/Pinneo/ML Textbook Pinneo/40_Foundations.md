| **SISSI Principle** | Acronym for "Shit I Should Eliminate" (S-I-S-E). A filter for decision-making used to immediately discard deals or complications that add unnecessary risk or stress. | Risk Management |

## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: Note Acquisition Valuation
* **Trigger:** IF offered a promissory note for purchase.
* **Action:** THEN perform the "Worst Case" analysis first, followed by the Yield calculation.
* **The Math:**
    1.  Ask: "If I have to foreclose, am I willing to own this property for the Purchase Price of the note?"
    2.  Calculate: Solve for Interest Rate (Yield) given: $PV$ (Purchase Price), $PMT$ (Monthly Payment), $N$ (Remaining Term).
* **Why:** You must be comfortable owning the underlying asset, as that is the ultimate security.

### Rule: The Discounted Note Windfall
* **Trigger:** IF you purchase a note at a discount (e.g., buy $400k face value for $300k) AND the borrower pays it off early (refinance/sale).
* **Action:** THEN the yield increases dramatically because the discount is realized immediately rather than over the amortization term.
* **Why:** Time compresses the profit. $100k profit over 20 years is good; $100k profit over 6 months is astronomical.

### Rule: The "Zero Down" Safety Valve
* **Trigger:** IF selling a property with "Zero Down" payment terms to attract buyers.
* **Action:** THEN require "Cross-Collateralization" on other assets the buyer owns.
* **Why:** It expands the buyer pool (marketing) while mitigating the lender's risk (security). If they default, you have recourse against their other equity.

### Rule: The Buffett Sort
* **Trigger:** IF analyzing a potential deal.
* **Action:** THEN sort into three baskets: 1) Yes, 2) No, 3) Too Difficult.
* **The Logic:** IF "Too Difficult" (Sharp Corners), THEN apply SISSI Principle (Eliminate).
* **Why:** Complicated deals often carry hidden risks that outweigh the returns.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Travel Because You Might Be Wrong**
* **Mindset:** Most people only know what they know and create a reality around it. Traveling (physically or intellectually) forces you to see other ways of operating.
* **Application:** Do not cling to "this is the right way." actively seek out contradictory information.

* **Principle:** **Control First**
* **Mindset:** Invest first in things you can control.
    1.  Control Yourself (Education, Discipline).
    2.  Control the Asset (Real Estate/Notes allow for forced appreciation/management, unlike stocks).

* **Principle:** **Cash Flow > Net Worth**
* **Mindset:** "Happiness is not having assets; it’s having a positive cash flow."
* **Application:** Shift focus from accumulation of property (landlord) to accumulation of income streams (lender).

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The $100k Windfall (The Yield Spike)
* **Situation:** Investor buys a $400,000 note (6% interest, 20-year term) for a discounted price of $300,000 to achieve a 14% yield.
* **Strategy Used:** Discounted Note Buying.
* **Outcome:** Six months later, the borrower sells the home and pays off the full face value ($400,000). The investor turns $300k into $400k in 6 months.
* **Key Lesson:** The "Wild Card" of note buying is early payoff; it converts a steady yield into a massive, immediate ROI.

* **Case Name:** The Vacant Land Liquidation
* **Situation:** Roger foreclosed on vacant land; market was soft, needed to sell.
* **Strategy Used:** Marketed as "Zero Down" to widen buyer pool + "Cross-Collateralization" for safety.
* **Outcome:** Found a strong buyer willing to pledge other real estate equity to secure the zero-down loan.
* **Key Lesson:** You can offer aggressive terms (Zero Down) if you secure the note with external assets (Cross-Collateralization).

* **Case Name:** The Arbitrage Play
* **Situation:** Investor finds a note yielding 20% but lacks personal capital.
* **Strategy Used:** Borrow funds at 8% (Bank or Private Lender with passbook savings) to buy the 20% note.
* **Outcome:** Investor pockets the 12% spread without using their own money.
* **Key Lesson:** You don't need money to make money; you need to negotiate the moving parts between those who have it (low yield) and where it is needed (high yield).
