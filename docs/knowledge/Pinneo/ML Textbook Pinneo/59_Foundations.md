## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Two-Column Negotiation
*   **Trigger:** IF dealing with an unsophisticated seller who is overwhelmed, stressed, or "worn out."
*   **Action:** THEN take out a sheet of paper, draw a line down the middle. Label left: "Where You Are Now." Label right: "Where You Could Be."
*   **The Math:**
    *   *Column 1 (Costs):* Taxes + Insurance + Maintenance + Utilities + Gas/Travel Costs = Monthly Bleed.
    *   *Column 1 (Subjective):* Stress, Fear of Vandalism, Traffic, Distance from Family.
    *   *Column 2:* Solutions that eliminate Column 1.
*   **Why:** Sophisticated metrics (Cap Rates, IRR) overwhelm simple sellers. Visualizing the removal of pain is a greater motivator than money alone.

### Rule: Calculating the Cash Flow Swing
*   **Trigger:** IF presenting an income-generating offer (Owner Financing) to a seller currently holding a money-losing asset.
*   **Action:** THEN present the value as the sum of Income + Savings.
*   **The Math:** $$Value = (Monthly Note Income) + (Current Monthly Holding Costs)$$
*   **Example:** $1,900 (Note Income) + $1,100 (Holding Costs Saved) = $3,000 Monthly Swing.
*   **Why:** It demonstrates a life-changing financial shift that is larger than the face value of the payment.

### Rule: The Retirement Fund Pivot
*   **Trigger:** IF a seller refuses to lower price because "This property is my retirement."
*   **Action:** THEN redefine retirement as "Principal Preservation" and offer Interest-Only payments.
*   **The Math:** $$Principal (Held Intact) \times Interest Rate = Spendable Cash Flow$$
*   **Why:** If they sell for cash and spend it, they deplete the principal. An interest-only note preserves the "Retirement Fund" forever (perpetuity).

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

*   **Principle:** The Unforgiving Minute (Kipling) / The Discretionary Eight
*   **Mindset:** Everyone gets 24 hours. No one is cursed with 23 or blessed with 25. Success is determined by how you utilize the 8 hours that are not Work or Sleep.
*   **Application:** Do not waste the discretionary time on low-value activities (TV, worrying about vacant houses). Negotiate to buy back the seller's time so they can use their Discretionary Eight better.

*   **Principle:** Quantifying the Subjective
*   **Mindset:** "Peace of mind has to be created by moving away from a difficult situation."
*   **Application:** Assign dollar values to non-monetary stressors (traffic, worry, distance). These are often stronger motivators than the actual asset price.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

*   **Case Name:** The 1-Acre Distressed Daughter
*   **Situation:** Daughter inherited a vacant house (tear-down condition) on 1 acre from ailing mother. Lives 45-90 mins away. Failed business, no income. Stressed by traffic and leaving mother in care facility. Holding costs ~$1,100/mo.
*   **Seller Constraint:** Fixed on $400,000 price (Land Value) as her "Retirement." Non-negotiable on price.
*   **Strategy Used:**
    1.  **Two-Column Negotiation:** Mapped out her "Bleed" vs. "Solutions."
    2.  **Owner Financing:** Met her price ($400k) but on terms.
*   **The Offer:**
    *   Price: $400,000
    *   Down: $20,000 (5%)
    *   Note: $380,000 @ 6% Interest Only ($1,900/mo)
*   **Outcome:**
    *   Seller Benefit: $3,000/mo Cash Flow Swing + Relief from subjective stress.
    *   Buyer Benefit: Control of sub-dividable land (3 lots) with low entry cost.
*   **Exit Strategies Considered:**
    1.  **Hold:** Eat negative cash flow ($12k/yr) for appreciation.
    2.  **Wrap:** Sell to handyman/couple for $500k ($20k down).
    3.  **Partner:** Builder covers negative cash flow for 50/50 split.
    4.  **Assign:** Flip contract to neighbor for $25k fee.
*   **Key Lesson:** Subjective negatives (stress/worry) are greater motivators than money. You can pay full price if the terms solve the seller's "pain" and provide cash flow.
