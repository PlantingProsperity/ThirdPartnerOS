## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Priority Triaging
* **Trigger:** IF you receive two calls simultaneously: 1. An existing investor/client, and 2. A "Bird Call" (New Lead).
* **Action:** THEN return the call to the existing investor/client FIRST.
* **Why:** "Unless you take care of the business you have, you don't really deserve new business."

### Rule: The "Gap" Hiring Formula
* **Trigger:** IF you feel you cannot "afford" an assistant.
* **Action:** THEN hire one anyway to handle "Firehouse" duties.
* **The Math:** `(Assistant Cost) < (Value of 3-5 additional face-to-face meetings per week)`
* **Why:** You afford help by freeing up time to generate revenue. You do not wait for revenue to hire help; you hire help to create the time to generate revenue.

### Rule: The Simultaneous Scribe Strategy
* **Trigger:** IF a verbal agreement is reached at a seller's table and you have a partner/assistant present.
* **Action:** THEN pull out *two* sets of blank forms. You write one agreement (e.g., Purchase & Sale), the assistant writes the other (e.g., Addendum/Assignment).
* **Why:** Speed and psychological momentum. Do not leave the table to "type it up." Strike while the iron is hot.

### Rule: The Contractor Termination Protocol
* **Trigger:** IF a contractor fails to show up on time AND fails to call in advance.
* **Action:** THEN fire them immediately upon their arrival.
* **Verbatim:** "Take your tools, put them back in your truck, and I'm hiring someone else."
* **Why:** Tolerance of unreliability creates a "slippery pole" in management.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** "The They is a You"
* **Mindset:** Do not look at a successful development and ask "Who are they?" Realize that "They" is just a person with two eyes and a brain who decided to act.
* **Application:** Stop observing the "Theys" of the world and form a group of "Yous" to become a "They."

* **Principle:** The Pilot's Checklist
* **Mindset:** 75% of business operations are usually "in your head." This is a failure point.
* **Application:** Every recurring task (Bird Letters, Rent Collection, Lease Signing) must have a physical checklist. You don't fly by memory; you fly by the list. This allows delegation.

* **Principle:** Face-to-Face Supremacy
* **Mindset:** "Our chances of doing that transaction go up 100% when we're together face-to-face."
* **Application:** Do not negotiate over the phone. Use the phone only to set the appointment.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** Setting expectations with a new contractor or vendor.
> **Verbatim Script:** "If you tell me you're going to do something, I need you to do it. If you tell me you're going to be on the job site at 9:00 on Tuesday morning, you need to be there at 9:00 on Tuesday morning. If, in fact, you are going to be late or if there's a change of plan, I need a call well in advance of that."
> **Tone:** Clear, direct, non-negotiable.

> **Scenario:** Confronting a contractor who missed a deadline without notice.
> **Verbatim Script:** "We'll probably be able to get to it tomorrow isn't good enough. I need to know if you can or you can't. Because if you can't, I have to get somebody else. It's okay with me if you can't, but it's not okay with me if you continue to put me off and never get the job done."
> **Tone:** Respectful but firm.

> **Scenario:** The Internal Monologue regarding success.
> **Verbatim Script:** "Who is they? Who is they and why aren’t you they? ... A they is a you. A they is a you that gets an idea, is committed to the idea, processes the idea, sets up a meeting with another you... And pretty soon a group of yous form a group that we call they."
> **Tone:** Challenging, introspective.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Bothell & Ballard Package Deal
* **Situation:** Greg received an informant lead on a vacant house in Ballard (Seattle). The owners lived in Bothell. The wife was emotionally attached to the Ballard home (vacant since 1977) and refused to sell, but the husband wanted to sell their primary residence in Bothell.
* **Strategy Used:**
    1.  **The Pivot:** Used the Bothell house (which Greg didn't want) as the bait to get a face-to-face meeting.
    2.  **The Leverage:** Once at the table, structured an offer to buy the Bothell house *only if* the Ballard house was included.
    3.  **The Tag-Team:** Once verbal yes was achieved, Greg and Liz pulled out blank forms and hand-wrote both contracts simultaneously at the dining room table.
* **Outcome:** Secured both properties.
* **Key Lesson:** Face-to-face meetings reveal motivations (Husband vs. Wife dynamics) that phone calls hide. Having a competent assistant allows for immediate execution (writing contracts on the spot).

* **Case Name:** The Whistler Negotiation
* **Situation:** Greg was at a retreat in Whistler; a building he had for sale received an offer. He could not be present.
* **Strategy Used:** **Remote Proxy Negotiation.** Greg debriefed Liz (assistant) on the strategy/counter-offer and empowered her to negotiate on his behalf based on his training.
* **Outcome:** Deal proceeded successfully without Greg's physical presence.
* **Key Lesson:** A "Firehouse" assistant must be more than a bookkeeper; they must be trained to duplicate the principal's negotiation capability to allow for true freedom.
