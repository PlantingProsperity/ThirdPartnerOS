## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Momentum Maintenance
* **Trigger:** IF you find a deal and start a renovation project...
* **Action:** THEN you must NOT stop marketing, prospecting, or hunting for money.
* **Why:** The business machine must run 24/7. Stopping creates a "start-stop" cycle that destroys momentum. Nike doesn't stop marketing just because they sold a shoe.

### Rule: The Blue Tape Protocol
* **Trigger:** IF the renovation is finished and you are ready to list...
* **Action:** THEN have a third party (not you) walk the house with a roll of blue masking tape to mark every flaw. Do not list until every piece of tape is removed.
* **Why:** The market punishes "almost done." The first impression must be flawless.

### Rule: The Financing Match
* **Trigger:** IF buying a long-term hold property...
* **Action:** THEN obtain long-term, low-interest fixed financing. NEVER use an Adjustable Rate Mortgage (ARM).
* **Why:** Short-term financing should not marry long-term hold objectives.

### Rule: The 10-Minute Gauge
* **Trigger:** IF viewing a potential property and meeting the seller...
* **Action:** THEN you must be able to make a "Go / No-Go" decision within 10 minutes.
* **Why:** Opportunities (Pheasants) do not wait 30 days for you to analyze them. You must know your comps/costs beforehand.

### Rule: Entrepreneurship Preservation
* **Trigger:** IF a seller claims the property has "potential" (e.g., "You can raise the rents," "You can add laundry")...
* **Action:** THEN base your offer ONLY on current collected income and expenses.
* **Why:** If you take the risk to execute the improvements, you keep the equity. Do not pay the seller for work you haven't done yet.

### Rule: Basement Vacancy Loading
* **Trigger:** IF analyzing income for basement units...
* **Action:** THEN apply a 10-20% vacancy factor (vs. 5-10% for above-ground).
* **Why:** Tenants (especially single women) dislike living underground due to safety and dampness concerns.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** The Business of Little Minds (Thomas Paine)
* **Mindset:** "I love the man that can smile in trouble... 'Tis the business of little minds to shrink."
* **Application:** When deals get stressful or the market gets tough, do not retreat. Smile, reflect, and gather strength.

* **Principle:** The Snake Metaphor
* **Mindset:** "Snakes only bite people that get in the range of the snake."
* **Application:** If your gut tells you a tenant or partner is untrustworthy, stay away. If you engage and get burned, it is your fault for getting in range, not the snake's fault for biting.

* **Principle:** The $10/Hour Trap
* **Mindset:** We have one resource: 24 hours a day. Doing job-site cleanup wastes high-value talent.
* **Application:** Hire out low-level labor immediately. Focus energy on finding deals and capital ($10,000/hour work).

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Insurance Surprise
* **Situation:** Greg bought a six-plex where the previous owner paid $1,200/year for insurance (grandfathered rate).
* **Strategy Used:** Greg assumed his costs would be similar without verifying.
* **Outcome:** The best quote Greg could get as a new owner was $9,000/year. A $7,800/year cash flow hit.
* **Key Lesson:** Never assume seller's expenses will transfer. Verify all costs as a *new* owner.

* **Case Name:** The Leaky Basement
* **Situation:** Investors buying properties with known basement leaks, planning to fix them during remodel.
* **Strategy Used:** Digging up the outside, furring out the inside.
* **Outcome:** The leak often returns. It is incredibly difficult to permanently cure.
* **Key Lesson:** If a basement leaks, treat it as storage/laundry only. Never budget it as finished living space.

* **Case Name:** The Swiss Ski Chalet (A-Frame)
* **Situation:** A property with a 16/12 pitch roof (A-Frame) in a normal residential neighborhood.
* **Strategy Used:** Attempting to remodel.
* **Outcome:** It remains a "Pig with Lipstick." You cannot change the fundamental shape/structure to conform to the neighborhood.
* **Key Lesson:** Do not buy "Odd Ducks" or properties with unfixable structural non-conformity.
