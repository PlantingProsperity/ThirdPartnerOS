## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Equity Screen (Bird Letter Targeting)
* **Trigger:** IF seeking high-equity leads for creative deal structuring.
* **Action:** THEN filter tax records for owners who have held the property for **10 years or more**.
* **Why:** High probability of equity allows for the full "tool bag" of negotiation (cash, owner carry, substitution of collateral).

### Rule: The "Terms" Pivot Script
* **Trigger:** IF a seller asks for a price near retail value (e.g., asking $95k on a $100k appraisal).
* **Action:** THEN pivot immediately to terms rather than price reduction.
* **The Script:** "I know right off the bat... that I can't pay cash and pay you that price... Would you be interested in a situation where I can pay you closer to that price, but rather than paying all cash, we worked out some kind of terms where I made **monthly payments** to you over a period of time?"
* **Why:** It tests for creativity/motivation without offending the seller with a lowball cash offer.

### Rule: The World Letter Ratio
* **Trigger:** IF sending a newsletter to your personal sphere ("World List").
* **Action:** THEN content must be roughly **75% Value/Entertainment** (trivia, quotes, local news) and **25% Business** (Need referrals/Success stories).
* **Why:** If it is 100% solicitation, it goes in the trash. It must be "friendly mail."

### Rule: The Referral Reciprocity Loop
* **Trigger:** IF a professional (e.g., Attorney) sends you a lead.
* **Action:** THEN feature their business/contact info in your next World Letter to your 300+ person list.
* **Why:** Public recognition triggers the "Reciprocity" instinct. They (and others watching) will send more leads to get the free exposure.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **The Coolidge Effect (Persistence)**
* **Mindset:** "Nothing in the world can take the place of persistence. Talent will not... Genius will not... Education will not... Persistence and determination are omnipotent."
* **Application:** When starting, assume a closing ratio of 1:40 or 1:100. Your job is not to close the deal; your job is to get in front of 100 people to find the 1 yes.

* **Principle:** **Reverse Engineering Success**
* **Mindset:** Define the specific requirements for Time and Mobility *first*, then build the business model to support it.
* **Application:** Avoid high-overhead, high-management models (like heavy rentals) if Mobility is the goal. favor "Churn" (Terms deals) to pay down "Hold" debt to achieve free-and-clear status faster.

* **Principle:** **Diversity Breeds Security**
* **Mindset:** Never rely on a single marketing channel.
* **Application:** Maintain a mix of "Augers": Direct Mail, Radio, Newspaper, Car Signs, and Referrals. If one dries up, the business survives.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** Pre-screening a seller on the phone who wants retail price.
> **Verbatim Script:** "I see you're asking $95,000 for your house. It's worth $100,000... I know right off the bat without ever going out to the house that I can't pay cash and pay you that price. I'm very interested in your house... But we're going to have to talk about an alternative if I'm going to be able to work with you."
> **Tone:** Direct but helpful. Setting boundaries early.

> **Scenario:** The Radio Ad (Personality Driven).
> **Verbatim Script:** "Hey friends, Dan Scott here. Do you own a house and need to sell right now?... Company president and former Clemson academic All-American linebacker Chad Carson says that you can sell your house in as-is condition for a fair price on the date of your choice... They make it easy for you to get more info... And in case you need a reason to call now, the first Cruise Control listener each month who sells Blue Ridge Home Buyers a house gets a free complimentary cruise to the Bahamas."
> **Tone:** High energy, local celebrity authority, scarcity (Bahamas trip).

> **Scenario:** Polite disqualification of a bad lead.
> **Verbatim Script:** "At this time, I'm probably not interested, Greg. But you know, I do have a friend who is doing some developments there in the Clemson area. Would you mind if I gave him your number and had him give you a call?"
> **Tone:** Helpful, preserving the relationship for future potential.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Toyota Camry Billboard
* **Situation:** Chad needed leads but had a limited budget.
* **Strategy Used:** Plastered "I Buy Houses" and phone number on his personal car (Toyota Camry).
* **Outcome:** Roughly 2 deals per year consistently, plus referral conversations at coffee shops.
* **Key Lesson:** Marketing requires suspending ego. If you are embarrassed to let people know what you do, you cannot succeed.

* **Case Name:** Carl Van Kalker (The Pace Setter)
* **Situation:** A college track athlete working on Greg's construction crew.
* **Strategy Used:** "The Grinder." He didn't just work; he challenged every new laborer to match him step-for-step.
* **Outcome:** He broke the will of others by simply outworking them.
* **Key Lesson:** You don't need to be the smartest or biggest. If you are the "first guy on the field, last guy to leave," you win.

* **Case Name:** The Attorney Referral Loop
* **Situation:** An attorney (Corinne Cannon) sent Chad one lead.
* **Strategy Used:** Chad featured her name, practice, and phone number in his "World Letter" (Newsletter) sent to 300 people.
* **Outcome:** She felt validated and sent more leads; other attorneys on the list saw this and began sending leads to get similar promotion.
* **Key Lesson:** Recognition is a free currency that buys loyalty.
