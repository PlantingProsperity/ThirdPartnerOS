## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The "Cliffhanger" Interrupt
* **Trigger:** IF a seller says a competitor is physically en route (or minutes away) to present a hard, all-cash offer.
* **Action:** THEN you must immediately stop the "Slow Dance" and ask point-blank for the competitor's number.
* **The Math:** N/A (Psychological calculation)
* **Why:** You cannot compete with an unknown variable under time pressure. You must quantify the threat to determine if you can beat it or reframe the vision before the pen hits paper.

### Rule: The Vacuum Leverage Shift
* **Trigger:** IF a developer builds *around* a holdout property rather than buying it.
* **Action:** THEN recognize that traditional leverage (necessity) is gone. The developer has proven they can exist without the property.
* **The Math:** $1,000,000 (Pre-construction offer) $\to$ $300,000 (Post-construction offer).
* **Why:** The "Highest and Best Use" has changed from "Essential Piece of the Puzzle" to "Convenience/Parking." The entrepreneur must create a new vision to restore value.

### Rule: The "Old School" Engagement
* **Trigger:** IF the seller is a "no BS," hardworking construction/trades type.
* **Action:** THEN drop all sales tactics. Be punctual, straightforward, and honor commitments absolutely.
* **Why:** This personality type values integrity over price. They were likely alienated by the "Corporate" behavior (missed appointments) of the developer.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** "Entrepreneurship is Seeing What's Not There."
* **Mindset:** Most people look at a 30x50 lot surrounded by 60ft walls and see a useless prison. An entrepreneur sees a unique anomaly with "scarcity value" precisely *because* of those constraints.
* **Application:** When analyzing a deal, do not stop at the physical constraints (zoning/walls). Look for the pressure points those constraints create for the neighbors.

* **Principle:** "Never Say No Until You Have To."
* **Mindset:** Maintain the "Slow Dance" and keep communication lines open, even if the deal looks impossible or the price is too high, until the moment of absolute decision.
* **Application:** Frequent "check-in" calls (every 2 weeks) to position yourself as the only viable alternative when the seller's patience with the primary buyer snaps.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Edith Macefield House (Part 1)
* **Situation:** A 1,500 sq ft lot (30'x50') in Seattle is surrounded by a massive commercial development (Ballard Blocks). The developer originally offered $1M+, which was refused. The owner (Edith) died, leaving the house to the construction superintendent.
* **The Conflict:** The developer wrapped the building around the house, leaving it in a "well." They are now offering the heir only $300k, claiming he has no other options.
* **Strategy Used:** Greg Pinneo identified the heir's frustration with the developer's arrogance (missed appointments). He engaged in a "Slow Dance" (regular check-ins) to build trust.
* **The Climax:** The developer is walking up the stairs with a $300k all-cash contract. The heir feels forced to sign. Greg is on the phone and must intervene immediately.
* **Outcome:** (Cliffhanger - To be continued in Part 2).
* **Key Lesson:** The developer's arrogance created an opening. Even when a developer holds all the "physical" cards (surrounding the property), they can lose the deal by failing to respect the human element (the seller).
