## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The General Contractor (GC) Arbitrage
* **Trigger:** IF [Investor intends to scale or build]
* **Action:** THEN [Obtain a General Contractor’s license immediately]
* **The Math:** `Savings = (Materials * 0.10 to 0.20) + (Escrow/Title Fee Discounts)`
* **Why:** Subcontractors give better pricing/service to a GC than "Joe Homeowner," and material discounts can pay for the license itself.

### Rule: The Ugly Elevation Strategy
* **Trigger:** IF [Submitting plans to a subjective Architectural Design Board]
* **Action:** THEN [Submit preliminary drawings with one elevation intentionally drawn "ugly" or flawed]
* **Why:** The Board will focus criticism on the obvious flaw. You accept the feedback to stroke their ego ("I don't know what I was thinking"), satisfying their need to contribute while distracting them from critiquing the rest of the project.

### Rule: The Fire Hydrant Radius
* **Trigger:** IF [Assessing a development site or new build]
* **Action:** THEN [Physically measure distance to nearest hydrant using a 1,000-foot tape measure]
* **The Math:** `IF Distance > 600 feet (typical code) THEN Add ~$11,000 to Budget`
* **Why:** If you are outside the radius, the city will force you to install a new hydrant at your expense before issuing a Certificate of Occupancy.

### Rule: Technical Drawing Order of Operations
* **Trigger:** IF [Subject to Design Review Board]
* **Action:** THEN [Do NOT pay for final engineering/technical drawings until aesthetic review is passed]
* **Why:** To prevent sunk costs on engineering for a design the Board might reject on aesthetic grounds.

### Rule: The Comprehensive Plan Forecast
* **Trigger:** IF [Reviewing Zoning]
* **Action:** THEN [Ask the city for the "Comprehensive Plan" (Comp Plan) forecast, not just the current map]
* **Why:** Current zoning may be High Density, but the Comp Plan may show an upcoming "Down-Zone" to Medium Density, devaluing the land.

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** **Living Deliberately**
* **Mindset:** "I know of no more encouraging fact than the unquestionable ability of man to elevate his life by a conscious endeavor." (Thoreau)
* **Application:** Do not rely on luck. Luck is finding a hydrant at 589 feet. Deliberate living is measuring it before you buy the land so you know the cost exists.

* **Principle:** **Real Estate is Construction**
* **Mindset:** "Nobody has ever built a building in the air."
* **Application:** Real estate is dirt, utility stubs, and frontage. You cannot be a successful investor without understanding the physical components (sewer depths, gas lines, soil permeability) that support the asset.

* **Principle:** **The Neighbor as Intelligence**
* **Mindset:** "I can learn more from an hour discussion with a neighbor than reading the entire development code."
* **Application:** Neighbors reveal the "soft data"—psychopaths next door, drainage issues, and the reality of dealing with the local county office.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The 1,000-Foot Tape Measure
* **Situation:** Greg completed a 4-lot duplex development. At final inspection, the city asked if a fire hydrant was within 600 feet. Greg didn't know.
* **Strategy Used:** Physically measured the distance from the building to the hydrant.
* **Outcome:** The distance was 589 feet. He saved ~$11,000 (cost of installing a new one).
* **Key Lesson:** He got lucky. Deliberate investors measure *before* construction. 12 feet of distance equaled $11,000 of liability.

* **Case Name:** The Wetland Ratio
* **Situation:** Greg needed to fill 400 sq. ft. of wetland to develop a property.
* **Strategy Used:** Compliance with County mitigation requirements.
* **Outcome:** Required to plant/rehab 2,800 sq. ft. of county property elsewhere (7:1 ratio).
* **Key Lesson:** Environmental impact costs are rarely 1:1; they are exponential.

* **Case Name:** The Down-Zoning Surprise
* **Situation:** Purchased property zoned High Density Multiple.
* **Strategy Used:** Relied on current zoning maps.
* **Outcome:** One year later, received notice the property was "Down-Zoned" to Medium Density.
* **Key Lesson:** Always check the "Comprehensive Plan" (forecast) regarding future zoning changes, not just the current status.
