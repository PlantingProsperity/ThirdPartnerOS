## 2. PROCEDURAL HEURISTICS (Logic & Algorithms)

### Rule: The Contractor Hall of Fame (The 1% Rule)
* **Trigger:** IF acting as a contractor or hiring one.
* **Action:** THEN execute the following hierarchy of behaviors.
* **The Math:**
    *   Show up on time = Top 10%
    *   + Do good work = Top 7%
    *   + Do it for the quoted price = Top 5%
    *   + Finish on time = Top 3%
    *   + Clean up the mess = **Top 1%**
* **Why:** The bar in the industry is incredibly low; basic discipline creates elite status.

### Rule: The Loan Committee Standard
* **Trigger:** IF preparing a binder for an institutional loan.
* **Action:** THEN provide one 4-inch binder for *every* member of the committee (don't make them share) and use large font.
* **The Math:** `Proposal Completeness = (Lender Needs * 10) + Tactile Samples`
* **Why:** If the lender has to ask for a single document after you leave, you have failed to "read their mind" and lost the initiative.

### Rule: The Pink Bow Protocol
* **Trigger:** IF a lender has specific lending criteria (e.g., 75% LTV, 1.2 DCR).
* **Action:** THEN structure the proposal to match those metrics exactly before entering the room.
* **The Math:** `Proposal Metrics == Lender Criteria`
* **Why:** "If this lender wants a pink bow, put a pink bow on your proposal! Give 'em what they want."

## 3. MENTAL MODELS (Philosophy)

* **Principle:** Identity Precedes Transaction.
* **Mindset:** "What you are in anything is what you are in everything."
* **Application:** A messy loan application implies a messy business operation. A meticulously organized binder implies a trustworthy borrower. You are not just selling a deal; you are selling your character.

* **Principle:** The Marden Recursion.
* **Mindset:** "We lift ourselves by our thought. We climb upon our vision of ourselves." (Orison Marden).
* **Application:** Before meeting a partner or lender, define your own status. If you think you are "stuck," you are. If you think you are "advancing," you are.

## 4. THE SCRIPT VAULT (Verbatim Dialogue)

> **Scenario:** The Team Authorization (Opening the door before they knock)
> **Verbatim Script:** "I hereby authorize you, Lender, to contact any member on my team, be it my accountant, my attorney, my financial planner, my employer... This is my team and I'm hereby authorizing you to contact any of them and have them release information about me that you might need."
> **Tone:** Transparent, confident, "Open Book."

> **Scenario:** Presenting to a Private Investor at a dining table
> **Verbatim Script:** "Do you have a moment to go through this with me? I'd like to give you a picture of who I am, what my business is about, and specifically why I'm asking you for money and how it all works."
> **Tone:** Respectful, professional, prepared.

> **Scenario:** Confronting a Defeated Partner
> **Verbatim Script:** "Don't speak for me. You may think you're stuck. You may think you have no options. You may think you need to retreat, but that's not how I think of myself. I don't think I'm stuck. I think there's a bright future, and I have no thoughts of retreat."
> **Tone:** Firm, differentiated, visionary.

## 5. CASE STUDY DATABASE (Structured Data)

* **Case Name:** The Partner's Retreat
* **Situation:** A business partner analyzed a project and concluded, "We are stuck, we must retreat."
* **Strategy Used:** Internal Re-framing (The Marden Principle). Greg refused to accept the partner's projection of failure onto his own identity.
* **Outcome:** Separation of mindsets; Greg realized they were on different pages regarding self-image.
* **Key Lesson:** What we think of ourselves is more important than the external reality.

* **Case Name:** The "Pat the Bunny" Loan Committee
* **Situation:** Presenting a complex remodel or new construction project to a conservative bank.
* **Strategy Used:** Sensory Lending. Included physical samples of carpet, tile, and cabinetry ("Pat the Bunny") alongside the numbers.
* **Outcome:** The Loan Committee spent the meeting passing the book around and feeling the samples rather than scrutinizing the risks.
* **Key Lesson:** Lenders are "three-year-olds in suits." Tactile evidence makes a project feel real and safe.

* **Case Name:** The Dog Papers Strategy
* **Situation:** Building the "Personal Profile" section of the Money Show.
* **Strategy Used:** Humanization. Included a picture of his Labrador Retriever *and* the dog's purebred papers.
* **Outcome:** Lenders felt they "knew" him.
* **Key Lesson:** Connect on a human level using hobbies/passions (planes, horses, dogs) to establish character before asking for money.
